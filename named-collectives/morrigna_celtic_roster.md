# The Morrígna — Celtic Roster

> See also: [morrigna_celtic.md](morrigna_celtic.md)

## Command hierarchy

No formal internal hierarchy is established in primary sources. The three (or four) members act as a collective without a single figure consistently designated as superior. *Cath Maige Tuired* names them together without ranking them. The individual Morrígan — who shares her name with the collective — is the most narratively prominent, but this reflects source emphasis rather than an attested leadership role.

The Morrígna operate within the broader Tuatha Dé Danann assembly without a named superior specific to the collective.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Badb**
- `[Specialization-major]` Washer at the ford — appears at river crossings before battles, washing the bloodied armor or weapons of warriors fated to die; a mortal who witnesses this knows they will not survive the coming fight
- `[Specialization-major]` Post-battle proclamation — after *Cath Maige Tuired*, chants a paired vision: first a prophecy of peace and prosperity, then an apocalyptic counter-vision of societal collapse; functions as a cosmic announcement audible to land and people
- `[Characteristic]` Crow/hooded crow form — her primary animal manifestation; *Badb Catha* ("battle crow") is her most common epithet
- `[Characteristic]` Kinship — daughter of Ernmas (divine mother of the war-goddess triad); sister of Macha and Morrígan; wife of Neit (god of battle) in most sources; daughter of Tethra (Fomorian king) in *Lebor Gabála Érenn*
- `[Characteristic]` Possible identity with Nemain — secondary scholarship proposes the two may be the same figure or overlapping aspects; both share the wife-of-Neit relationship and battlefield-panic roles; Hennessy noted differing parentage across sources suggesting distinctness; no primary source resolves this — see Nemain entry

**Macha Mong Ruad** ("red-haired")
- `[Strength]` Sole female High King — the only woman recorded as High King of Ireland in the traditional king-list; defeated rivals Díthorba and his sons in battle (*Lebor Gabála Érenn*)
- `[Specialization-major]` Sovereignty founding — forced her defeated enemies to build Emain Macha (Navan Fort) using her brooch as the measure; ruled for 14 years

**Macha (daughter of Ernmas)**
- `[Characteristic]` Morrígna member — the divine war-goddess aspect; daughter of Ernmas alongside Badb and the Morrígan; acts with the collective in battle contexts (*Cath Maige Tuired*, *Lebor Gabála Érenn*)
- `[Characteristic]` Epithet *Grian Banchure* ("Sun of Womanfolk") — appears as a secondary name in some sources
- `[Strength]` Sovereignty over Ulster — Emain Macha and Ard Macha (Armagh) are named for this figure; land-sovereignty goddess of the Ulster territory

**Macha (wife of Cruinniuc)**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Sovereignty curse — coerced into racing the king's horses while heavily pregnant; wins, collapses at the finish line, gives birth to twins, and curses all Ulstermen to suffer the debility of a woman in labour for five days and four nights at their time of greatest need; the curse endures for nine generations and directly explains the Ulstermen's vulnerability during *Táin Bó Cúailnge* (*Noínden Ulad*)
- `[Characteristic]` Horse association — supernatural speed even while pregnant; named daughter of Sainrith mac Imbaith in primary sources

> **Note on the three Machas:** Primary sources present these as three separate narrative figures who share the name. No medieval primary text explicitly identifies them as the same being — the "three Machas" framework is a construction of modern scholarship (Proinsias Mac Cana discusses three; Gregory Toner discusses four). Each identity is listed separately here per the multiple-identity members rule: the primary-source record treats them as distinct narratives.

**Morrígan (individual)**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Shape-shifting — attested forms in *Táin Bó Cúailnge*: crow/raven, eel, wolf, heifer, old woman; all deliberate, narrative-purpose transformations
- `[Specialization-major]` Battle prophecy — foretells death, doom, or victory before and during conflict; announces outcomes aloud
- `[Strength]` Sovereignty conferral — can bless or withhold a king's legitimacy over territory, cattle, and people
- `[Fault]` Vulnerable in animal form — in *Táin Bó Cúailnge*, Cú Chulainn wounds her in each of the three animal forms she takes (eel, wolf, heifer); wounds persist until she is unknowingly healed by him while disguised as an old woman
- `[Characteristic]` Samhain ford meeting — meets the Dagda at a ford on Samhain; the occasion for her pledge of aid against the Fomorians (*Cath Maige Tuired*)

**Nemain**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Battle-cry panic — a single cry kills 100 warriors in a night by fright alone; confounds armies, causing soldiers to turn on their own forces
- `[Characteristic]` Possible identity with Badb — secondary scholarship only; no primary source explicitly equates or definitively separates them; both share the wife-of-Neit relationship and overlapping battle-panic roles; Hennessy noted differing parentage in some primary texts suggesting distinctness — see Badb entry

> **Note on Nemain:** Nemain appears in *Táin Bó Cúailnge* and *Lebor na hUidhre* as a distinct war spirit acting alongside the Morrígna but is not named as a collective member in any primary text. Her formal inclusion here reflects secondary scholarly association. The possible conflation with Badb is likewise from secondary scholarship — no primary source equates them, and some primary sources give them different parentage.
