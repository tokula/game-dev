# Amatsukami — East Asian Roster

> See also: [amatsukami_east_asian.md](amatsukami_east_asian.md)

## Command hierarchy

Takamagahara is governed by Amaterasu-Ōmikami as supreme ruler, with Takamimusubi-no-Kami acknowledged as co-authority in the Nihon Shoki (the two jointly direct Ninigi's descent). The five kotoamatsukami precede Amaterasu in cosmic order but withdraw into concealment and play no further governing role. The Seven Divine Generations (Kamiyo Nanayo) are subordinate to the kotoamatsukami in the creation sequence and operate under their mandate. Ninigi descends from Takamagahara as Amaterasu's emissary and stands below her in the heavenly hierarchy.

```
Kotoamatsukami (5) — primordial, concealed after emergence
  └─ Mandate to create → Kamiyo Nanayo (Seven Divine Generations, 12 kami)
       └─ Izanagi & Izanami — final creative pair; produce the Three Precious Children
            ├─ Amaterasu-Ōmikami — supreme ruler of Takamagahara
            │    └─ (with Takamimusubi) → Ninigi — emissary to earth
            ├─ Tsukuyomi-no-Mikoto — ruler of the night (estranged)
            └─ Susanoo-no-Mikoto — ruler of the seas (exiled)
```

**Source priority:** Primary sources (Kojiki, Nihon Shoki, Engishiki, Man'yōshū) are the authoritative origin. All entries below derive from these texts. Derivative sections exist for cross-reference only.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Ame-no-Koyane-no-Mikoto**
- `[Specialization-minor]` Divination — performed the deer-bone and cherry bark divination (futomani) during the Ama-no-Iwato council to determine how to restore the world's light
- `[Characteristic]` Ancestral function — enshrined as the tutelary kami of the Nakatomi (later Fujiwara) clan; continued ritual role at court long after the mythological age

**Ame-no-Minakanushi-no-Kami**
- `[Specialization-signature]` First of all kami — emerged before any other being, embodying the cosmic center (御中主, the August Center); no other kami precedes or generates him
- `[Characteristic]` Complete withdrawal — alone among the kotoamatsukami in having no further narrative function whatsoever after emergence; he does not even issue the mandate that Takamimusubi and Kamimusubi later exercise

**Ame-no-Uzume-no-Mikoto**
- `[Specialization-major]` Ecstatic performance — her wild, semi-unclothed kagura dance before the cave caused the assembled kami to laugh, which provoked Amaterasu's curiosity and drew her out; this act restored light to the world
- `[Strength]` Diplomatic boldness — when Ninigi's procession is blocked by Sarutahiko on the heavenly road, Ame-no-Uzume confronts and negotiates with him, earning her the surname Sarume-no-Kimi

**Amaterasu-Ōmikami**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Solar sovereignty — as the sun goddess, her presence or absence directly determines whether the world has light; her retreat into Ama-no-Iwato plunged all creation into darkness, enabling evil kami to run rampant
- `[Specialization-major]` Divine investiture — bestows the Three Sacred Treasures (Yata no Kagami mirror, Yasakani no Magatama jewel, Kusanagi-no-Tsurugi sword) on Ninigi as the regalia of earthly sovereignty; these remain the imperial regalia of Japan
- `[Modifier]` Retreated into Ama-no-Iwato cave in grief and anger at Susanoo's rampage — *triggered by Susanoo's destruction of her rice paddies and weaving hall*; required elaborate ritual performance to emerge

**Ame-no-Tokotachi-no-Kami**
- `[Characteristic]` Eternal continuity — name explicitly encodes the concept of heavenly permanence (tokotachi, "standing forever"); functions as a structural principle rather than an agent
- `[Characteristic]` Some Nihon Shoki variants pair Ame-no-Tokotachi with Kunitokotachi as a creation dyad, though the Kojiki treats them separately in different generational tiers

**Futodama-no-Mikoto**
- `[Specialization-minor]` Ritual assembly — organised the sacred offerings used in the Ama-no-Iwato ritual: the eight-span mirror, hard-blue jewels, white and soft hemp cloth, and deer-bone divination materials

**Izanagi-no-Mikoto**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Island creation — together with Izanami, stirred the primordial ocean with Ame-no-Nuboko and raised the first land (Onogoro Island); subsequently created the eight major islands of Japan and countless kami from their bodies
- `[Specialization-major]` Purification birth — after fleeing Yomi and performing misogi (ritual purification), Izanagi's washing of his left eye produced Amaterasu, his right eye produced Tsukuyomi, and his nose produced Susanoo — the Three Precious Children born from a single purification act
- `[Modifier]` Pursued by Izanami and the shikome (ugly women of Yomi) after fleeing the underworld — *consequence of breaking the prohibition against looking at Izanami's decaying form*; sealed Yomi with the Chigaeshi no Ōkami (boulder of return)

**Izanami-no-Mikoto**
- `[Specialization-major]` Co-creation of land and kami — produced the islands and numerous elemental kami (including mountains, seas, winds, trees, and fire) from her body alongside Izanagi
- `[Fault]` Death by fire birth — Izanami died giving birth to the fire god Kagutsuchi; her death initiated mortality into the world and established Yomi as the realm of the dead
- `[Specialization-major]` Ruler of Yomi — after death, became the sovereign of the underworld; threatened to kill one thousand people per day in retaliation for Izanagi's flight, establishing death as a permanent fact of existence
- `[Modifier]` Estrangement from Izanagi — decreed an irreconcilable separation after he saw her rotting form and fled — *consequence of Izanagi's broken promise not to look at her in Yomi*

**Kamimusubi-no-Kami**
- `[Specialization-major]` Life restoration — her (or their; gender varies by source) musubi power directly revived Ōkuninushi after he was killed by the Yasobito; the mechanism is divine creative generation applied to the dead
- `[Characteristic]` Female in most sources; gender unmarked in the Kojiki text itself — the Nihon Shoki is more explicit about femininity

**Kunitokotachi-no-Kami**
- `[Characteristic]` Confirmed Amatsukami — Kunitokotachi appears first in the Kamiyo Nanayo sequence within the Takamagahara creation narrative and is classified as a heavenly kami; the "kuni" in the name encodes the cosmological earth-principle he embodies (the eternal standing of the land as concept), not a classification as an earthly Kunitsukami. The Nihon Shoki treats him as male, the Kojiki as hitorigami/genderless. Later Yoshida Shinto (Yoshida Kanetomo, 15th century) identified him with Amenominakanushi as the single primordial deity of the universe — a theological reinterpretation that underscores his standing as supreme rather than subordinate.

**Ninigi-no-Mikoto**
- `[Specialization-major]` Heavenly descent (tenson kōrin) — descends from Takamagahara to the peak of Takachiho in Hyūga province, accompanied by five divine attendants and bearing the Three Sacred Treasures; this act transfers Amatsukami authority to the earthly line
- `[Characteristic]` Ancestor of the Imperial House — through his great-grandson Jimmu Tennō (first legendary emperor), Ninigi's line becomes the foundation of the Japanese imperial dynasty

**Susanoo-no-Mikoto**
- `[Specialization-major]` Storm mastery — god of storms and the sea; his weeping and mourning (longing for his dead mother Izanami) caused mountains to wither, seas to rise, and evil kami to proliferate
- `[Specialization-major]` Orochi-slaying — slew the eight-headed serpent Yamata no Orochi by getting it drunk; discovered Kusanagi-no-Tsurugi (later gifted to Amaterasu) in its tail
- `[Strength]` Poetry — composed the first waka poem in Japanese literary tradition ("Ya kumo tatsu / izumo yaegaki…") upon building a palace at Suga after slaying Yamata no Orochi
- `[Modifier]` Exiled from Takamagahara by collective decree of the Amatsukami — *consequence of destructive rampage that drove Amaterasu into Ama-no-Iwato cave*; after exile became associated with the earthly realm (Izumo)

> **Note:** The Nihon Shoki attributes the killing of the food deity Ukemochi to Tsukuyomi; the Kojiki's parallel episode involves Susanoo killing Ōgetsuhime. Scholars treat these as parallel variants of the same "food from the body of a slain deity" myth type — a template also found in other agrarian mythologies — rather than descriptions of the same event. The two versions serve distinct narrative functions: the Nihon Shoki version explains why day and night are permanently separated (Amaterasu refuses to look at Tsukuyomi); the Kojiki version explains the origin of cultivated crops and sericulture (emerging from Ōgetsuhime's body after Susanoo's exile). Neither primary source equates the two episodes. Both are recorded separately under their respective deity entries.

**Takamimusubi-no-Kami**
- `[Specialization-major]` Co-governance — in the Nihon Shoki, Takamimusubi acts as co-equal authority with Amaterasu in dispatching Ninigi; several emissaries are sent on their joint command
- `[Characteristic]` Most narratively active kotoamatsukami — unlike the other three who withdraw entirely, Takamimusubi continues to issue commands and receive reports in both the Kojiki and Nihon Shoki

**Tsukuyomi-no-Mikoto**
- `[Specialization-major]` Lunar sovereignty — god of the moon and ruler of the night realm; given dominion over the night by Izanagi at the same moment Amaterasu received the day
- `[Modifier]` Eternally estranged from Amaterasu — *consequence of slaying Ukemochi (保食神) for serving food produced from her own body, which Amaterasu found abhorrent*; Amaterasu declared she would never look upon Tsukuyomi again — hence the sun and moon occupy opposite sides of the sky (Nihon Shoki only; the Kojiki does not include this narrative)

> **Note:** Tsukuyomi receives substantially less narrative coverage in primary sources than Amaterasu or Susanoo. The Kojiki mentions Tsukuyomi only briefly at birth; the Ukemochi killing and its consequences are attested only in the Nihon Shoki. Tsukuyomi's role as a full deity with a distinct cult is less well-documented than the other two Precious Children.

**Umashiashikabihikoji-no-Kami**
- `[Characteristic]` Reed-shoot emergence — name invokes the image of vigorous reeds sprouting from the primordial mud; embodies the first stirring of life-force in creation before form was established; hitorigami
