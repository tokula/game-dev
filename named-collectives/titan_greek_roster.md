# Titans — Greek Roster

> See also: [titan_greek.md](titan_greek.md)

## Command hierarchy

Cronus, youngest son of Uranus and Gaia, served as king of the Titans after overthrowing his father. Among siblings, each Titan governed an independent cosmic domain without a formal internal sub-hierarchy. After the Titanomachy, Zeus holds absolute authority over the surviving Titans; the imprisoned are guarded in Tartarus by the Hecatoncheires under his directive.

**Non-Titan superiors:** Uranus (deposed sky-father) and Gaia (earth-mother) stand above the generation by lineage but are not themselves Titans.

**Source priority:** Primary sources (Hesiod's *Theogony* and *Works and Days*; Aeschylus's *Prometheus Bound*; Apollodorus's *Bibliotheca*) are authoritative. Secondary sources exist for cross-reference only.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Atlas**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Condemned to sustain the heavens on his shoulders at the western edge of the world — a cosmological burden assigned to him alone, permanent and physically immovable (*Theogony* ll. 517–520)
- `[Fault]` Fixed at his station; cannot leave the western horizon

**Cronus**
- `[Modifier]` Devoured his children at birth to prevent the prophecy of his own overthrow; failed when Rhea concealed Zeus — *Theogony*, Hesiod, ll. 459–467
- `[Modifier]` Imprisoned in Tartarus after the Titanomachy — *Theogony*, Hesiod, ll. 717–735

**Menoetius**
- `[Fault]` Extreme recklessness and hubris singled out explicitly even among Titans (*Theogony* l. 514)
- `[Modifier]` Struck by Zeus's thunderbolt and cast into Erebus — not Tartarus — *Theogony*, Hesiod, ll. 514–516

**Mnemosyne**
- `[Specialization-minor]` Personification of memory; the entire tradition of Greek poetry and music flows through her lineage: all nine Muses are her daughters by Zeus (*Theogony* ll. 915–917)

**Prometheus**
- `[Specialization-major]` Stole fire from the gods and gave it to humanity — an act of civilisational gift-giving beyond any natural or trained capacity (*Theogony* ll. 562–569)
- `[Modifier]` Bound to a rock in the Caucasus where an eagle consumes his liver daily; it regenerates each night; the punishment is eternal unless rescinded — decreed by Zeus for the theft of fire — *Theogony*, Hesiod, ll. 521–525; later freed by Heracles (*Bibliotheca*, Apollodorus)
- `[Characteristic]` His name means "forethought"; he predicted the Titans would lose the Titanomachy and sided with Zeus accordingly (*Prometheus Bound*, Aeschylus)

**Themis**
- `[Specialization-minor]` Embodiment of divine law and the proper order of assembly; her authority over sacred law persisted unchanged through the Titan-to-Olympian succession; later the second consort of Zeus and mother of the Horai (Seasons) and Moirai (Fates)
