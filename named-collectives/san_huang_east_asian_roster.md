# Three Sovereigns — East Asian Roster

## Command hierarchy

No formal internal hierarchy among the San Huang is documented in primary sources; the three are conceptually co-equal as divine founders. Collectively they precede all subsequent human and cosmic order. Above them in the Chinese cosmological hierarchy sits Heaven (天, Tiān) and the abstract cosmic order itself. The Five Emperors inherit and continue their era but do not rank above them in mythological status.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

> **Named Collective note:** For Named Collective profiles, this section is mandatory — do not omit it even when individuals closely resemble the collective profile. Every named member should appear here with any trait that differs from (or adds to) the collective baseline. The individual distinctions are the core game-design value of a Named Collective entry.

**Fuxi 伏羲**
- `[Characteristic]` Serpent or dragon lower body — depicted in Han dynasty art as half-human, half-serpent; frequently shown paired with Nüwa
- `[Signature]` Invented the Eight Trigrams (八卦, Bāguà) — the cosmological framework underlying the I Ching; no other being in Chinese mythology is credited with an act of equal structural importance to divination and cosmological thought
- `[Strength]` Taught fishing, trapping, and animal husbandry — civilizational gift distinct from Shennong's agricultural domain

**Nüwa 女娲**
- `[Characteristic]` Serpent or dragon lower body — identical form to Fuxi; in Han-era art the two are regularly depicted as entwined serpent-bodies, symbolising the paired creative principle
- `[Signature]` Created humanity from yellow clay — fashioned the first humans by hand; later accounts say she used a cord drawn through mud to create the multitudes; *Chuci* (Tian Wen), *Liezi*
- `[Signature]` Repaired the broken sky — after the catastrophe (attributed in some sources to Gonggong striking Mount Buzhou), Nüwa smelted five-coloured stones to patch the sky and straightened the world-pillar; *Liezi*
- `[Characteristic]` Established the institution of marriage — credited with creating formal marriage rites that regulated human reproduction; *Fengsu Tongyi* (2nd century CE, PD)

**Shennong 神農**
- `[Characteristic]` Human body, bull or ox head (人身牛首) — physically the most distinct of the three canonical Sovereigns; attested in *Shanhaijing*
- `[Signature]` Personal pharmacological testing — personally tasted every plant and herb to determine medicinal and toxic properties; is described in later tradition as having encountered 70 poisons in a single day; his testing forms the empirical foundation of Chinese herbal medicine
- `[Strength]` Invented the plow (耒耜) and taught systematic agriculture — organizer of crop cultivation; distinct from Fuxi's hunting/fishing gifts
