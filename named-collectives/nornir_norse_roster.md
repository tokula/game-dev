# Nornir — Norse Roster

> See also: [nornir_norse.md](nornir_norse.md)

## Command hierarchy

The three great Norns (Urðr, Verðandi, Skuld) act in concert throughout primary sources. No formal rank is stated among the three; they perform identical functions collectively. Urðr gives her name to their cosmic seat (Urðarbrunnr), but no explicit seniority is asserted in primary sources.

No Norn is documented as answering to any external authority.

**Source priority:** Primary sources (folklore, mythology, religious texts, historical accounts) are the authoritative origin. Games and fiction derive from them. Always populate `— Primary sources` first and most completely. Derivative sections exist for cross-reference only — they should never displace or reframe the original cultural record.

**Section naming rule:** Used `— Primary sources` because the Nornir have a real-world Norse mythological base.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Skuld**
- `[Characteristic]` Named in both the Nornir group (*Völuspá* stanza 20) and the Valkyrie roll-call (*Grímnismál* stanza 36) — the only figure to appear in both categories across primary sources; whether this reflects dual function, functional identity between the two groups, or loose categorisation in oral tradition is unresolved in scholarship; see also [valkyrie_norse_roster.md](../races/valkyrie_norse_roster.md)

**Urðr**
- `[Characteristic]` The cosmic seat of all three Norns bears her name exclusively — Urðarbrunnr ("Urðr's Well") — suggesting she was the primary or earliest-named figure in this tradition; her name is etymologically identical to Anglo-Saxon *wyrd* (fate), indicating her name became the concept itself in adjacent Germanic traditions — *Völuspá*; *Gylfaginning*

**Verðandi**
- No individual distinctions from the collective baseline are attested in primary sources beyond her membership in the triad and her name's meaning ("that which is becoming / present happening").
