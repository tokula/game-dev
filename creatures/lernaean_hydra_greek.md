# Lernaean Hydra

**Native name / Etymology:** Λερναῖα Ὕδρα (Lernaía Hýdra) — from Λέρνα (Lerna, the swamp near Argos where she dwelt) + ὕδρα (hýdra, "water serpent", from ὕδωρ hydor, "water")

**Source Layer:** Greek

**Primary origin:** Greek mythology — *Theogony*, Hesiod, c. 700 BCE; elaborated across multiple classical authors

**Biological Form:** Aberration/Eldritch

**Entity type:** Unique creature

**Scale:** Regional — terrorises the region around Lerna; her venom poisons local waters and her raids destroy nearby villages; the threat is bounded to the Argolid

**Parent species:** None — offspring of Typhon and Echidna, both unique primordial beings; sibling to Cerberus, Orthrus, and Chimera

**Disposition:** Predatory — emerges from her lair in the spring of Amymone to raid neighbouring villages; attacks anyone who approaches

**Gender(s):** Female

## One-line lore summary

A many-headed water serpent lurking in the swamps of Lerna whose severed heads regenerate twofold — defeated by Heracles only by cauterising each stump before it could regrow.

## Physical description

All sources agree on: massive serpentine body with multiple heads; a lair in the marsh at Lerna; lethal venom in both breath and blood. Head count varies significantly by source:

- Alcaeus (lyric, c. 6th cent. BCE, fragmentary): nine heads
- Simonides (lyric, c. 5th cent. BCE, fragmentary): fifty heads
- Apollodorus, *Bibliotheca*: nine heads (the most widely cited canonical figure)
- Hesiod, *Theogony*: does not give a specific count; ancient bronze fibulae depicting the labour (c. 700 BCE) show six heads

One head was immortal and could not be killed — only physically severed and buried under a heavy rock between Lerna and Elaius.

> **Note:** Nine heads is the canonical figure in the classical prose tradition (Apollodorus) and is the count present in every detailed prose account of the labour. The fifty-head figure (Simonides) and bronze-art variants likely reflect early epic superlative rather than a structural claim.

## Specializations

- `[signature]` Head regeneration — two new heads sprout from every severed stump; the regeneration is immediate and automatic; no other creature in the mythological record possesses this property; severing alone worsens the threat
- `[signature]` Immortal central head — one head was intrinsically indestructible; it could only be removed and permanently contained, never killed; even after Heracles defeated her, the head had to be buried under a boulder

## Strengths

- Extreme toxic potency — venom, blood, and breath were all lethal to humans; even the scent of the blood was described as deadly in ancient accounts
- Multiple simultaneous attack vectors — several heads can strike in different directions at once

## Characteristics

- Lair-bound — inhabits the spring of Amymone and surrounding swamp at Lerna; emerged to raid but is rooted to that location
- Member of legendary monster lineage: sibling to Cerberus, Orthrus, and Chimera through Typhon and Echidna

## Faults / Weaknesses

- Regeneration requires a gap — if each severed stump is immediately cauterised by fire before a new head sprouts, the regeneration is prevented; Heracles defeated her only by having Iolaus burn each neck stump after every cut
- Immortal head requires physical disposal — the surviving head, though indestructible, is still a physical object that can be removed and buried under sufficient weight; containment without destruction was the only viable resolution

## Modifiers

None — her nature and role are intrinsic, not imposed.

## Allies / Rivals / Enemies

| Faction | Standing | Notes | Source |
|---------|----------|-------|--------|
| Hera | Superior | Hera raised the Hydra specifically to destroy Heracles; she also sent a giant crab (Cancer) to distract Heracles during the fight — Heracles crushed it; Hera later placed both the Hydra and the crab as constellations | *Bibliotheca*; tradition |
| Heracles | Enemy | Slew the Hydra as his second labour with Iolaus' help; dipped his arrows in her blood afterward, creating a supply of lethally poisoned weapons | *Bibliotheca* |

## Ecology

**Prey:** Inhabitants of the Lerna region — raids neighbouring villages; livestock and humans

**Predators:** None in primary sources; Heracles defeated her through collaborative strategy rather than superior predation
