# Orthrus

**Native name / Etymology:** Ὄρθρος (Orthros) — from ὄρθρος, "dawn" or "the twilight just before sunrise"; the word denotes the liminal period at the boundary of night and day; Latinised as Orthrus or Orthus

**Source Layer:** Greek

**Primary origin:** Greek mythology — *Theogony*, Hesiod, c. 700 BCE; Apollodorus, *Bibliotheca*, c. 1st–2nd century CE

**Biological Form:** Beastfolk

**Entity type:** Unique creature

**Scale:** Personal — guards a specific herd of cattle on a remote island; his scope and threat are entirely bounded to the isle of Erytheia

**Parent species:** None — offspring of Typhon and Echidna, both unique primordial beings; sibling to Cerberus, the Lernaean Hydra, and the Chimera

**Disposition:** Territorial — attacks any intruder who approaches Geryon's cattle on sight; loyal to his master and purposeful in his aggression

**Gender(s):** Male

## One-line lore summary

A two-headed monstrous hound stationed on the far-western isle of Erytheia to guard the legendary red cattle of Geryon — killed by Heracles at the outset of the tenth labour.

## Physical description

Hesiod (*Theogony*) names Orthrus as a son of Typhon and Echidna and explicitly describes him as a two-headed dog. No source gives him scales, a serpent tail, unusual colouring, or features beyond the doubled head. His physical profile is that of a giant, two-headed watchdog of supernatural stock — closely related to Cerberus but with two heads rather than three (or fifty in Hesiod's early count for his sibling).

> **Note:** Primary sources are brief in their physical description of Orthrus. Only the two heads and his parentage are explicitly attested in Hesiod. Later sources confirm his role and fate without elaborating on his appearance.

## Specializations

None explicitly attested beyond what his primordial lineage implies.

## Strengths

- Two-headed vigilance — simultaneous perception in two directions; impossible to approach unseen from behind
- Supernatural size and ferocity — as an offspring of the primordial monster Typhon, significantly more dangerous than any natural dog

## Characteristics

- Guardian role — stationed specifically as a watchdog for Geryon's red cattle; his aggression is directed and purposeful, not indiscriminate
- Member of legendary monster lineage: sibling to Cerberus, the Lernaean Hydra, and the Chimera through Typhon and Echidna
- Progenitor of the Sphinx and the Nemean Lion — Hesiod's *Theogony* (ll. 326–329) names Orthrus explicitly as the father; the Evelyn-White 1914 Loeb translation reads: *"Echidna, as they say, was subject in love to Orthus and brought forth the deadly Sphinx which destroyed the Cadmeans, and the Nemean Lion"* (see Note on sources for the mother debate)

## Faults / Weaknesses

- Mortal — killed outright by Heracles with a single blow of his club; unlike his sibling Cerberus (who was captured and returned alive), Orthrus was slain and not restored; no special method was required to kill him

## Modifiers

None — his nature and role are intrinsic, not imposed.

## Ecology

**Prey:** Intruders approaching Geryon's cattle — aggressive guardian who attacks anyone who comes near the herd; not described as hunting for food

**Predators:** None in primary sources; Heracles killed him in direct combat without any special strategy being required

> **Note on sources:** Orthrus's paternity is not in dispute — Hesiod's *Theogony* (ll. 326–329) names him explicitly as the male partner in the conception of the Sphinx and the Nemean Lion. The ambiguity is about the **mother**. Evelyn-White's 1914 Loeb translation (PD) reads the subject as Echidna, making the mother Orthrus's own mother — an incestuous coupling. M.L. West's 1966 Oxford edition reads the immediately preceding subject (Chimaera) as carrying forward into that clause, making the mother Chimaera — Orthrus's own sibling. Either reading involves incest. Apollodorus does not repeat the fatherhood claim.
