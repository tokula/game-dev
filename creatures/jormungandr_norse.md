# Jörmungandr

**Native name / Etymology:** Jörmungandr (Old Norse) — "vast encircler" or "great monster"; from *jörmun-* "vast, mighty" (a poetic intensifier also used in Jörmunrekr, Jörmungrund) + *gandr* "monster, magic staff, wand." Also known as Miðgarðsormr ("Midgard Serpent") and the World Serpent.

**Source Layer:** Norse

**Primary origin:** Norse mythology — Prose Edda, Snorri Sturluson (c. 1220) and Poetic Edda (anonymous, c. 10th–13th century)

**Biological Form:** Aberration/Eldritch

**Entity type:** Unique creature

**Scale:** Cosmic — physically encircles the entirety of Midgard, biting its own tail; its coiled grip is the natural boundary of the world; its release precipitates Ragnarök and the flooding of the world's shores; its venom fills the air at Ragnarök, killing the most powerful of the Æsir

**Parent species:** None — unique offspring of Loki and the jötunn Angrboða; sibling to Fenrir (wolf) and Hel (ruler of the dead)

**Disposition:** Inert during the cosmic age — it coils in the world-ocean, biting its own tail, exerting no active threat; becomes catastrophically hostile at Ragnarök when it releases its tail and comes ashore

**Gender(s):** Male — consistently referred to with masculine pronouns in primary sources

## One-line lore summary

The monstrous serpent child of Loki who encircles Midgard in the depths of the world-ocean, destined to rise at Ragnarök, fill the sky with venom, and exchange killing blows with Thor.

## Physical description

A serpent of immense size — large enough to encircle the entirety of Midgard and bite its own tail while doing so. Described in primary sources as an *ormr* (Old Norse: serpent, worm, or dragon). No specific physical features beyond its size are attested in the Eddas; no horns, wings, or limbs are described.

Thor's fishing expedition (*Hymiskviða*) establishes that it can be pulled toward the surface on a strong enough line and looked upon directly: Thor and Jörmungandr stared at each other across the water before Hymir cut the line in fear. The encounter implies a distinct head, eyes capable of meeting a god's gaze, and a jaw capable of biting an ox-head used as bait.

Its venom is the most consistently attested physical trait: at Ragnarök it sprays poison across air and sea as it comes ashore, and its venom kills Thor after Thor walks nine steps away from the serpent's corpse.

> **Note:** The description of Jörmungandr creating tidal waves or crushing mountains does not appear in the Poetic or Prose Edda. Such elaborations are likely later synthesis or modern popular conflation. Only the attested primary-source traits are recorded here.

## Specializations

- `[signature]` **Worldcoiling** — encircles Midgard in its entirety and bites its own tail; this is the natural boundary of the mortal world, not merely a feat of strength. Its release of its own tail is one of the signs that Ragnarök has begun
- `[major]` **Cosmic venom** — its poison is potent enough to kill Thor, the strongest of the Æsir, after a direct blow from Mjölnir; at Ragnarök it saturates the air and sea, poisoning all it reaches

## Strengths

- Scale sufficient to hold the world in its coils — implies physical force beyond any individual being in the Norse cosmos
- Endurance: survived multiple encounters with Thor (the fishing episode in *Hymiskviða*; an earlier contest at Útgarðr disguised by Útgarða-Loki's illusion) without being slain

## Characteristics

- Ouroboros posture — its default resting state is to bite its own tail while encircling Midgard; this is simultaneously a physical fact and a cosmological symbol of the world's containment
- Inert for the cosmic age: it does not hunt, threaten, or interact with the world until Ragnarök; its presence is a latent condition rather than an active one
- Fated death: it will die at Ragnarök; the Norse cosmological framework makes this outcome fixed

## Faults / Weaknesses

- Susceptible to Mjölnir — Thor kills it at Ragnarök with a direct blow from his hammer, though the venom ensures a mutual death
- Earlier encounters demonstrate it can be physically brought toward the surface against its will (Thor's fishing) — not entirely beyond mortal and divine reach

## Modifiers

- **Cast into the ocean by Odin** — Odin threw Jörmungandr into the world-sea surrounding Midgard upon learning of Loki's monstrous offspring; this exile is the source of its cosmological role as world-encircler — *Prose Edda (Gylfaginning)*

## Ecology

**Prey:** N/A — no feeding behaviour is attested during the cosmic age; it holds its own tail in its mouth while encircling Midgard; at Ragnarök it sprays venom but is not described as consuming anything

**Predators:** Thor is its destined killer; no natural predator is described during the cosmic age
