# Kui

**Native name / Etymology:** 夔 (Kuí) — etymology unconfirmed in primary sources; the character's form is ancient and no authoritative classical gloss survives

**Source Layer:** East Asian

**Primary origin:** Chinese mythology — *Shanhaijing* (Classic of Mountains and Seas), anonymous, compiled c. 475–100 BCE

**Biological Form:** Beastfolk

**Entity type:** Unique creature

**Scale:** Regional

**Parent species:** None

**Disposition:** Force of nature — no aggression toward humans is described; manifests as a coastal weather phenomenon and cosmic omen

**Gender(s):** Sexless / N/A — no gender indicated in primary sources

## One-line lore summary

A one-legged, thunder-voiced celestial ox of the Eastern Sea whose hide, stretched over a war drum, could be heard for 500 *li* and filled all beneath heaven with dread.

## Physical description

The *Shanhaijing* describes Kui as resembling an ox but hornless and with only one foot; its body is blue-black; its eyes shine like the sun and moon; its cry sounds like thunder; when it enters or exits the sea it brings wind and rain. It dwells on Mount Liubo in the Eastern Sea.

> **Note:** The *Shanhaijing* is a compiled anthology absorbing diverse regional traditions; Kui's description varies between its sections. All variants agree on the single-foot trait; head type, facial features, and body colour differ between passages.

## Specializations

- `[signature]` Thunder-voice — produces a roar audible up to 500 *li* (approximately 250 km); attested as sufficient to induce supernatural dread across vast forces and to immobilise opposing armies in legendary battle
- `[major]` Weather manifestation — its entry into and exit from the sea is accompanied by wind and rain

## Strengths

- Luminous eyes described as shining like the sun and moon, visible at great range

## Characteristics

- Single-limbed — one foot is the defining trait consistent across all *Shanhaijing* variants
- Aquatic-terrestrial — inhabits a mountain in the Eastern Sea, moving between land and water
- Solitary — no social structure or conspecifics attested

## Faults / Weaknesses

- Mortal — killed by the Yellow Emperor (Huangdi), who harvested its hide for a war drum
