# Oceanids — Greek Roster

> See also: [oceanid_greek.md](oceanid_greek.md)

## Command hierarchy

No formal hierarchy governs Oceanids as a class. They are subject to their parents Oceanus and Tethys (both Titans) and ultimately to the Olympians. Many Oceanids became consorts of Olympians or Titans and thereby entered specific divine power structures individually. Styx holds a unique position of universal authority through her waters' role in binding divine oaths; she is respected above all other Oceanids in this function, though she holds no general command over her sisters.

**Source priority:** Hesiod's *Theogony* (ll. 349–362) is the primary authority. Apollodorus's *Bibliotheca* and Ovid's *Metamorphoses* supplement individual myths.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Calypso**
- `[Specialization-major]` Gift of immortality — Calypso offers Odysseus immortality and eternal youth if he remains with her; no other mortal in primary sources is offered this by a being other than an Olympian; the offer is genuine (*Odyssey* Bk 5.135–136)
- `[Modifier]` Compelled to release Odysseus — Hermes delivers Zeus's order; Calypso is powerless to refuse despite her independent divine status; the episode demonstrates that even remote divine beings (on distant Ogygia) are subject to Olympian authority — *Odyssey*, Homer, Bk 5

**Metis**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Embodiment of divine cunning wisdom — Metis is the personification of *mētis* (μῆτις), the quality of cunning counsel and practical wisdom; she advised Zeus during his struggle against Cronus; her wisdom is literally incorporated into Zeus (and by extension Athena) when he swallows her
- `[Modifier]` Swallowed by Zeus — Zeus swallowed Metis on Gaia's advice, to prevent the prophecy that Metis's son would surpass Zeus in power; she continues to advise Zeus from within — *Theogony*, Hesiod, ll. 886–900

**Perseis**
- `[Characteristic]` Mother of the two great sorcerers — through Circe and Aeetes, Perseis is grandmother of Medea (the witch of the Argonaut cycle); her lineage dominates the mythological tradition of magical inheritance and transformation

**Styx**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Oath-binding authority — Styx is the mechanism by which all divine oaths are sealed and enforced; an Olympian who swears by Styx and breaks the oath suffers "breathless sickness" for a year and then nine years of exclusion from divine assembly; even Zeus is bound by it (*Theogony*, Hesiod, ll. 793–806)
- `[Strength]` First to side with Zeus — Styx brought her children (Nike, Bia, Kratos, Zelos) to aid Zeus in the Titanomachy before other Oceanids acted; Zeus honoured this loyalty by making her waters the supreme divine sanction (*Theogony*, Hesiod, ll. 383–403)
