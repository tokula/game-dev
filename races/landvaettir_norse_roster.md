# Landvættir — Norse Roster

> See also: [landvaettir_norse.md](landvaettir_norse.md)

## Command hierarchy

No hierarchy governs the Landvættir collectively. Each spirit is sovereign within its own territory and has no authority over Landvættir elsewhere. No named leader or common authority is documented.

**Source priority:** Primary sources (Landnámabók, Heimskringla) are authoritative.

## Named individuals — Primary sources

Landvættir are not identified by personal names in primary sources; they are described by their geographical territory and the form they take. The four primary documented guardians are the Iceland cardinal spirits from Heimskringla:

| Name | Source | Rank / role | Notes |
|------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Eastern Guardian (Dragon) | Heimskringla (*Óláfs saga Tryggvasonar*) | Guardian of eastern Iceland (Vopnafjörður) | Manifested as a great dragon accompanied by serpents, toads, and lizards; turned back the sorcerer sent by Harald Bluetooth |
| Northern Guardian (Eagle) | Heimskringla (*Óláfs saga Tryggvasonar*) | Guardian of northern Iceland (Eyjafjörður) | Manifested as a huge eagle accompanied by many smaller birds; similarly repelled the scout |
| Western Guardian (Bull) | Heimskringla (*Óláfs saga Tryggvasonar*) | Guardian of western Iceland (Breiðafjörður) | Manifested as a great bull accompanied by a herd of various beasts |
| Southern Guardian (Mountain Giant) | Heimskringla (*Óláfs saga Tryggvasonar*) | Guardian of southern Iceland | Manifested as a mountain giant carrying an iron staff, accompanied by other wights; the final guardian encountered |

> **Note:** These beings are identified by location and manifestation form, not personal names. Treating the descriptive labels above as proper names would misrepresent the source material. Primary sources do not name individual Landvættir.
