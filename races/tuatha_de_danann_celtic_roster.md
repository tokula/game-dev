# Tuatha Dé Danann — Celtic Roster

> See also: [tuatha_de_danann_celtic.md](tuatha_de_danann_celtic.md)

## Command hierarchy

The Tuatha Dé Danann are organized around a High King (*rí*) supported by a chief druid, war-goddesses, specialized craftspeople, and a healer. Legitimate kingship requires physical wholeness and Ireland's fertility flourishing under the king's rule — a physically blemished king must abdicate.

**High King** (Nuada → Bres, illegitimate → Nuada restored → Lugh) — supreme commander in war and peace; binds the land's prosperity  
**Chief Druid** (The Dagda, *Eochu Ollathair*) — spiritual and magical authority; overseer of sovereignty and abundance  
**War-goddess / sovereignty** (The Morrígan, Badb, Macha) — battle sorcery, prophetic declaration, and land-sovereignty  
**Three Gods of Craft** (Goibniu, Creidhne, Luchtaine) — weapons production and material battlefield support  
**Healer** (Dian Cecht) — battlefield restoration; maintains the healing spring

No formal hierarchy below these offices is documented in primary sources.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Aengus (Mac ind Óc)**
- `[Specialization-minor]` Temporal manipulation — tricked the Dagda into granting permanent residence at Brú na Bóinne by asking for "a day and a night," which Irish idiom can mean all time (*De Gabáil in t-Sída*); whether this constitutes a rule-breaking ability or a contractual cleverness is left open by the primary text
- `[Characteristic]` Love and youth deity; associated with affairs crossing mortal-divine boundaries; dwells in the most sacred Neolithic mound

**Badb**
- `[Specialization-major]` Battle confusion — can create magical darkness, panic, and disorientation within enemy formations; warriors struck by her presence flee or turn on each other (*Cath Maige Tuired*)
- `[Characteristic]` Manifests as a hooded crow or as a washing woman at a ford (foretelling imminent death) — a distinct manifestation set from the Morrígan's documented shapes

> **Note:** Badb, Macha, and the Morrígan are sometimes described in secondary scholarship as three aspects of a single triple goddess. This equation is not explicitly stated in the primary sources and is a scholarly framework layered over texts that treat them as distinct sisters. Each is listed here as a separate individual.

**Bres**
- `[Modifier]` Half-Fomorian parentage — father Elatha (Fomorian prince); his Fomorian allegiances overrode his Tuatha Dé kingship obligations, leading him to enslave his own people to Fomorian tribute-collectors — *origin: Ériu's union with Elatha (Lebor Gabála Érenn)*
- `[Fault]` Satire-exposed — his misrule (denial of feasts, enslavement of craftspeople, failure to maintain hospitality) made him uniquely vulnerable to the formal satire composed by Coirpre the Bard; the satire stripped his kingship without military action

**The Dagda (Eochu Ollathair)**
- `[Specialization-major]` Magic club (*lorg mór*) — one end kills; the opposite end revives the slain; no other primary-source individual possesses a weapon with this dual-function (*Cath Maige Tuired*)
- `[Specialization-major]` Inexhaustible cauldron (*Coire Ansic*) — never empties; one of the four treasures brought from the mythical cities; provides unlimited sustenance to the Tuatha Dé (*Lebor Gabála Érenn*)
- `[Characteristic]` Described as fat and wearing a short tunic in *Cath Maige Tuired* — a comedic portrayal likely functioning as a deliberate subversion of the idealized-king archetype; his power is not diminished by the description

**Dian Cecht**
- `[Specialization-major]` Healing spring (*Loch Luibe* / the Tuatha Dé's well) — fallen warriors cast into the spring overnight were restored to full health; sustained Tuatha Dé fighting capacity throughout the Second Battle of Mag Tuired (*Cath Maige Tuired*)
- `[Fault]` Killed his son Miach out of jealousy when Miach's healing arts surpassed his own (*Cath Maige Tuired*) — the only attested case of a Tuatha Dé member committing violence against a direct kinsman from professional pride

**Étaín**
- `[Specialization-minor]` Reincarnation cycle — transformed into a butterfly by the jealous Fúamnach, swept away by wind, swallowed, and reborn as a mortal woman with no memory of her divine origin; the multi-life cycle is unique among named Tuatha Dé members (*Tochmarc Étaíne*)
- `[Modifier]` Transformation into butterfly — *imposed by Fúamnach through magical jealousy; not intrinsic to Étaín's nature*

**Lugh (Lámfhada)**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Master of all crafts — uniquely among the Tuatha Dé, Lugh claims excellence in every art simultaneously (warrior, smith, poet, harpist, sorcerer, physician, cupbearer, metalworker); the Tuatha Dé gatekeeper refuses him entry until he names a craft they lack — Lugh replies that no single member combines all of them (*Cath Maige Tuired*)
- `[Characteristic]` Half-Fomorian lineage through his mother Ethniu (Balor's daughter); his Fomorian blood is the prophetically necessary condition for Balor's defeat — the prophecy required a *grandson* of Balor
- `[Specialization-major]` Spear of Lugh (one of the four treasures) — one of the four magical treasures; never misses (*Lebor Gabála Érenn*)

**Macha**

> **Note:** Three distinct figures named Macha appear in primary sources: (1) Macha Mong Ruad (Red-Maned Macha), a sovereignty queen and warrior; (2) Macha, daughter of Ernmas, a war-goddess and sister of the Morrígan; (3) Macha, wife of Cruinniuc, the mortal-allied figure who cursed the Ulstermen. No primary source explicitly identifies these as the same being. The equation of all three is a secondary scholarly inference. Each tradition is noted in the sources column of the Named individuals table; they are not collapsed here.

**Manannán mac Lir**
- `[Specialization-major]` Otherworld passage — governs the boundaries between the mortal world and the sídhe; can grant or deny access to the otherworld for mortals and divine beings alike
- `[Characteristic]` Absent from both major battles (Mag Tuired); his domain is travel, boundary, and the sea rather than direct military conflict; his power operates outside the war-cycle

**The Morrígan**
- `[Specialization-major]` Shapeshifting — transforms into crow, wolf, eel, and heifer forms, all attested in primary sources (*Cath Maige Tuired*, *Táin Bó Cúailnge*); cannot be replicated by other races through training
- `[Specialization-major]` Victory declaration — her formal proclamation from a hilltop after the Second Battle of Mag Tuired functions as a binding cosmic statement that seals the outcome; the narrative treats it as determinative, not descriptive (*Cath Maige Tuired*)
- `[Characteristic]` Proposes alliance and combat with Cú Chulainn (Ulster Cycle) — simultaneously antagonist and potential patron to mortal heroes; her relationships with mortals are unpredictable rather than uniformly hostile

**Nuada Airgetlám ("Silver Hand")**
- `[Modifier]` Disqualified from kingship while physically imperfect — sovereignty law binds all Tuatha Dé kings to physical wholeness; Nuada's severed hand forced his abdication; Dian Cecht created a functional silver replacement (earning Nuada his epithet); Miach later restored the flesh hand (an act that killed Miach when Dian Cecht destroyed his work) — *origin: sovereignty law intrinsic to the Tuatha Dé compact with Ireland*
- `[Characteristic]` His injury drives multiple narrative arcs: the silver arm, Miach's rivalry with Dian Cecht, and the vacancy of the kingship that enabled Bres's usurpation

**Ogma (Oghma)**
- `[Specialization-minor]` Attributed inventor of Ogham writing — the Ogham script is attributed to Ogma in *Sanas Cormaic* as a form of cryptic, magically charged inscription tied to language and power; treated in the source as an invention, not a discovered natural phenomenon
