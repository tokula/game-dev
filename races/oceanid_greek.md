# Oceanids

> See also: [oceanid_greek_roster.md](oceanid_greek_roster.md)

**Native name / Etymology:** Ὠκεανίδες (*Ōkeanídes*); singular Ὠκεανίς (*Ōkeanís*); daughters of Ὠκεανός (*Ōkeanos*, Oceanus), the Titan river encircling the earth; Oceanus's name is of uncertain etymology, possibly from a pre-Greek substrate or from an ancient word for "swift."

**Source Layer:** Greek

**Primary origin:** Greek mythology — Hesiod's *Theogony* (c. 700 BCE) defines them as the 3,000 daughters of the Titans Oceanus and Tethys (ll. 349–362); individual Oceanid myths documented throughout Apollodorus's *Bibliotheca* and Ovid's *Metamorphoses*.

**Biological Form:** Humanoid (semi-divine)

**Entity type:** Race/Species

**Gender(s):** Female

**Common archetype/role in fiction:** Nature spirits

## One-line lore summary

The three thousand daughters of Oceanus and Tethys — the living embodiments of every river, cloud, spring, and raindrop on earth, whose lineage binds the boundless ocean to every drop of water in the mortal world.

## Specializations

- `[major]` **Universal water domain** — collectively, the Oceanids personify all bodies of fresh water on earth and all sources of rainfall; their domain is broader than that of any other water-spirit class; individual Oceanids govern rivers, underground springs, clouds, and rain (*Theogony*, Hesiod, ll. 349–362).
- `[minor]` **Oath-binding authority (Styx)** — Styx, most famous of the Oceanids, is the river by which the Olympians swear unbreakable oaths; her authority over divine promises exceeds that of any other Oceanid and is respected even by Zeus (*Theogony*, Hesiod, ll. 776–806).

## Strengths

- As daughters of the world-encircling Oceanus, they have reach across all water sources on earth — a Oceanid's influence extends wherever water flows from her domain.
- Many Oceanids become consorts of Olympians and Titans, producing important divine and semi-divine lineages: Metis bears Athena, Doris mothers the Nereids, Clymene mothers Atlas and Prometheus.
- Some Oceanids ascend beyond nature-spirit roles into cosmic functions: Metis (cunning wisdom) and Styx (divine law) hold authority that shapes Olympian governance.

## Characteristics

- 3,000 in number per Hesiod's *Theogony* — by far the largest class of nymphs and one of the largest divine categories in Greek mythology.
- Daughters of the Titans Oceanus and Tethys; their brothers, the Potamoi (river gods), are equally numerous.
- Function overlaps with Naiads (freshwater nymphs of specific springs and rivers): Oceanids tend to personify broader water systems (entire rivers, the ocean itself), while Naiads tend individual springs and pools; the distinction is imprecise and the two categories overlap in ancient sources.
- Titan lineage: unlike most nymph classes, Oceanids are second-generation Titan offspring, giving them divine pedigree between the Protogenoi and the Olympian era.
