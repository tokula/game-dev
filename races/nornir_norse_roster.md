# Nornir — Norse Roster

> See also: [nornir_norse.md](nornir_norse.md)

## Command hierarchy

The three great Norns (Urðr, Verðandi, Skuld) hold the highest position among all Nornir by virtue of their permanent seat at Urðarbrunnr — the cosmological centre — and their role as stewards of Yggdrasill. No formal rank is stated among the three; they act in concert throughout primary sources.

Lesser Norns are subordinate by scope — they govern individual fates at births rather than the fate-architecture of the cosmos as a whole. No Norn is documented as answering to any external authority.

**Source priority:** Primary sources (folklore, mythology, religious texts, historical accounts) are the authoritative origin. Games and fiction derive from them. Always populate `— Primary sources` first and most completely. Derivative sections exist for cross-reference only — they should never displace or reframe the original cultural record.

**Section naming rule:** Used `— Primary sources` because the Nornir have a real-world Norse mythological base.

## Named individuals — Primary sources

| Name | Source | Rank / role | Notes |
|------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Urðr | *Völuspá* st. 20; *Gylfaginning* | One of the three great Norns | Name means "that which was / wyrd"; etymologically identical to Anglo-Saxon *wyrd* (fate); considered the eldest of the three |
| Verðandi | *Völuspá* st. 20; *Gylfaginning* | One of the three great Norns | Name means "that which is becoming / present happening" |
| Skuld | *Völuspá* st. 20; *Grímnismál* st. 36; *Gylfaginning* | One of the three great Norns; also named as a Valkyrie in *Grímnismál* | Name means "that which shall be / debt / obligation"; the only figure named in both the Nornir and Valkyrie roll-calls in primary sources — see note below |

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Skuld**
- `[Characteristic]` Named in both the Nornir group (*Völuspá* stanza 20) and the Valkyrie roll-call in *Grímnismál* stanza 36 — the only figure to appear in both categories across primary sources; whether this reflects dual function, functional identity between the two groups, or loose categorisation in oral tradition is unresolved in scholarship; see also [valkyrie_norse_roster.md](valkyrie_norse_roster.md)
