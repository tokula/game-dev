# Nornir — Norse Roster

> See also: [nornir_norse.md](nornir_norse.md)

## Command hierarchy

No hierarchy among Nornir is documented. Urðr, Verðandi, and Skuld operate collectively at the Well of Urðr; the many other Nornir act independently at individual births across the Nine Worlds. All Nornir are subordinate to no other being — their authority is the highest in Norse cosmology.

**Source priority:** Primary sources (Poetic Edda, Prose Edda) are authoritative.

## Named individuals — Primary sources

| Name | Source | Rank / role | Notes |
|------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Urðr | *Völuspá*; Prose Edda (*Gylfaginning*) | Norn of the Past | Name means "what once was" (past of *verða*); tends the Well of Urðr at the base of Yggdrasil; the Æsir hold their daily assembly at her well |
| Verðandi | *Völuspá*; Prose Edda (*Gylfaginning*) | Norn of the Present | Name means "what is becoming" (present participle of *verða*); carves fate alongside her sisters into the World Tree |
| Skuld | *Völuspá*; Prose Edda (*Gylfaginning*) | Norn of the Future | Name means "what shall be / what ought to be" (from *skulu*, obligation and future necessity); also listed among the Valkyries in *Völuspá* — the only figure documented in both roles |

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Skuld**
- `[Characteristic]` Dual role — listed in *Völuspá* among both the Nornir and the Valkyries; the only named figure in primary sources documented in both capacities. Whether this represents a single being with two functions, a shared name between two distinct figures, or a scribal ambiguity is unresolved.
