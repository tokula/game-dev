# Naiads — Greek Roster

> See also: [naiad_greek.md](naiad_greek.md)

## Command hierarchy

No formal hierarchy governs Naiads as a class. Individual Naiads are subject to the authority of their local river god (typically a Potamos, himself a son of the Titans Oceanus and Tethys) and ultimately to the Olympians. Each Naiad is sovereign over her specific spring, stream, or pool within that larger framework. No single authority coordinates all Naiads.

**Source priority:** Primary sources (Homer's *Iliad* and *Odyssey*; Hesiod's *Theogony*; Ovid's *Metamorphoses*; Plato's *Phaedrus*) are authoritative.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Amymone**
- `[Characteristic]` Encountered both attacker and protector at the same spring — the satyr's assault and Poseidon's rescue both occur at the same site; the spring Amymone's discovery reveals becomes the sacred spring at Lerna; her story structures the pattern of Naiad encounters (threat → divine rescue → spring revealed)

**Arethusa**
- `[Specialization-minor]` Underground travel — Artemis transforms Arethusa into an underground stream that travels beneath the sea from the Peloponnese to Sicily; she retains her identity as she flows; the myth reflects real phenomena of underground spring systems in the Mediterranean
- `[Modifier]` Transformed into a spring permanently by Artemis — not a voluntary transformation; Arethusa's mortal/nymph form is subsumed into the spring; she continues to exist as flowing water — *Metamorphoses*, Ovid, Bk 5.572–641

**Io**
- `[Modifier]` Transformed into a cow — by Zeus (to conceal the affair) or by Hera (in jealousy); accounts differ; she wanders the world, tormented by a gadfly sent by Hera, before being restored to human form in Egypt — *Bibliotheca*, Apollodorus, 2.1.3
- `[Characteristic]` Ancestress of Heracles — after restoration in Egypt, Io bears Epaphus by Zeus; her lineage eventually produces the Danaids, Perseus, and Heracles; she is one of the most genealogically significant Naiads
