# Veliona

> See also: [veliona_baltic_roster.md](veliona_baltic_roster.md)

**Native name / Etymology:** Veliona (Lithuanian) — from *vėlė* ("shade, soul of the dead"), with the feminine augmentative suffix *-ona* suggesting "great soul" or "mother of souls"; shares a root with Velnias (lord of the dead) and the Vėlės (collective souls); also preserved in the place-name Veliuona (town in Jurbarkas district, Lithuania), where an attested pre-Christian cult site stood

**Source Layer:** Baltic

**Primary origin:** Lithuanian oral tradition — *dainos* (folk songs) and fragmentary 16th–17th century Christian records; place-name evidence at Veliuona (Lithuania) indicates a significant pre-Christian cult presence; no single primary text gives her extended treatment

**Biological Form:** Spirit

**Gender(s):** Female

## One-line lore summary

A Lithuanian underworld goddess of the dead — guardian of souls, underground waters, and buried treasures — whose cult at Veliuona suggests deep pre-Christian roots that the surviving written record only partially preserves.

## Specializations

- `[major]` Governs underground waters, streams, caves, and subterranean vaults — controls what passes through or rests in the earth's hidden spaces; no other being shares this precise domain in Lithuanian cosmology
- `[minor]` Psychopomp guidance — leads souls to their place in the afterlife; a function complementary to Giltinė's death-delivery role (Giltinė releases the soul; Veliona receives and places it)

## Strengths

- Controls access to buried wealth and underground treasures; her favor or disfavor determines what the earth yields or withholds
- Associated with agriculture and animal health through her governance of underground water and soil
- Guardian role over the Vėlės — souls under her care are protected from malevolent interference

## Characteristics

- Dwelling: underground spaces — caves, underground streams, barrows, subterranean vaults
- Occupies a middle position in the underworld hierarchy: between Velnias (the ruler) and the Vėlės (the ruled) — a caretaker goddess whose authority is custodial rather than sovereign
- Her cult site at Veliuona (on the Nemunas River) suggests a significant regional priesthood before Christianization
- Associated with both the dead and the living world's agricultural cycles through her water-domain

## Faults / Weaknesses

- Her attested authority is subordinate — she operates within Velnias's domain and cannot override his sovereignty over the dead
- Thin primary attestation means her role boundaries are unclear; where her authority ends and Velnias's begins is not resolved in surviving sources

## Modifiers

- Operates within Velnias's overarching domain — *divine hierarchy; origin: Lithuanian folk cosmology; Velnias is lord of the underworld; Veliona's guardianship is a subordinate function within that realm*

> **Note on sources:** Veliona is the most thinly attested of the eight beings in this Baltic chthonic batch. Her profile is substantially a reconstruction based on name etymology, place-name evidence, and functional position within Lithuanian cosmology. No single primary text gives her extended treatment comparable to the coverage Velnias receives in Łasicki (1582) or the Vėlės receive in the *raudos* and *dainos* tradition. The profile should be understood as a best-supported reconstruction, not a fully documented deity. Game designers should treat her role as more speculative than the other Baltic entries.
