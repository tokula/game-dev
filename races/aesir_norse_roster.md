# Æsir — Norse Roster

> See also: [aesir_norse.md](aesir_norse.md)

## Command hierarchy

Odin (Allfather, Hárr) holds supreme authority among the Æsir as king of Ásgarðr and ruler of Valhöll. Thor is the foremost warrior and protector of gods and mankind. Týr holds authority over law and single combat. Heimdall maintains the standing sentinel role at Bifröst. Freyja (Vanir-born, counted Æsir) leads in matters of seiðr. No fully formalised internal hierarchy is documented; the Æsir govern by assembly (þing) with Odin's voice carrying highest weight.

**Source priority:** Primary sources (folklore, mythology, religious texts, historical accounts)
are the authoritative origin. Games and fiction derive from them. Always populate
`— Primary sources` first and most completely. Derivative sections exist for cross-reference

**Section naming rule:** Use `— Primary sources` when the race has a real-world folkloric or

entirely by the IP filter and never reach this label. The label signals whether you're citing
a cultural tradition or an open creative work.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Odin**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Sacrificed an eye to Mímir's well in exchange for cosmic wisdom — *Völuspá*
- `[Specialization-major]` Hung on Yggdrasill self-wounded for nine nights to obtain the runes — *Hávamál*
- `[Specialization-major]` Sends his two ravens Huginn and Muninn across all Nine Worlds daily; they return with knowledge of everything seen and heard — *Grímnismál*
- `[Modifier]` Doomed by fate to be devoured by Fenrir at Ragnarök despite his foreknowledge and all precautions — *Völuspá*

**Loki**
- `[Specialization-major]` Full shapeshifting including biological sex change — takes mare form, is impregnated by the stallion Svaðilfari, and gives birth to the eight-legged horse Sleipnir — *Gylfaginning*
- `[Modifier]` Blood-oath of brotherhood with Odin binds him to the Æsir despite Jötunn birth — *Lokasenna*
- `[Modifier]` Bound in a cave beneath a mountain with a serpent dripping venom onto his face until Ragnarök, after orchestrating Baldr's death — *Völuspá*; *Gylfaginning*

**Baldr**
- `[Modifier]` Conditional invulnerability — Frigg secured oaths from all things in the world not to harm him, save the mistletoe; Loki exploited the gap — *Gylfaginning*

**Freyr**
- `[Fault]` Traded away his magical self-fighting sword to obtain Gerðr as his bride; this leaves him weaponless at Ragnarök when he faces Surtr — *Skírnismál*; *Gylfaginning*

**Týr**
- `[Fault]` Lost his right hand as surety when Fenrir was bound with Gleipnir — a permanent individual wound, not a race trait — *Gylfaginning*
