# Æsir — Norse Roster

> See also: [aesir_norse.md](aesir_norse.md)

## Command hierarchy

Odin (All-Father) sits at the apex of Ásgarðr's hierarchy as king of the Æsir. Frigg, as his wife and queen, is explicitly his equal in authority per the Prose Edda. The twelve principal male Æsir hold individual domains without a fixed ranked chain of command among themselves; authority is exercised through personal power and divine domain. No formal military or bureaucratic hierarchy below Odin is documented in primary sources.

```
Odin (All-Father, king)
├── Frigg (queen; equal in authority to Odin)
├── Thor (champion against Jötnar)
├── Baldr (most beloved; no battlefield command role)
├── Heimdallr (watchman of Bifröst; sentinel function)
├── Týr (war and justice)
└── [remaining Æsir by domain, no fixed rank among them]
```

**Source priority:** Primary sources (Poetic Edda, Prose Edda, sagas) are authoritative. Game and literary sections are derivative cross-references only.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Odin**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Sacrificed one eye at Mímir's well to gain cosmic wisdom — a unique self-mutilation for transcendent knowledge beyond normal divine capacity
- `[Specialization-major]` Gained the runes by hanging himself on Yggdrasil for nine nights without food or water — this ordeal is specific to him and available to no other being by default
- `[Modifier]` Travels in disguise under many names ("Grímnir," "Gangleri," "Wanderer," etc.), hiding his divine nature through deliberate identity shifts — *self-initiated practice attested across multiple Eddic poems*

**Loki**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Full biological shapeshifting — can change species and sex completely (became a mare and bore Sleipnir; became a fly, a salmon, an elderly woman); this exceeds any other Æsir's recorded shapeshifting in scope
- `[Modifier]` Bound beneath the earth with serpent venom dripping onto his face until Ragnarök, as punishment for engineering Baldr's death — *imposed collectively by the Æsir, Völuspá and Gylfaginning*

**Freyr**
- `[Fault]` Gave away his self-wielding sword for the giantess Gerðr — permanently lost his primary weapon; will enter Ragnarök unarmed and fall to Surtr as a direct consequence — *Skírnismál*

**Baldr**
- `[Modifier]` Conditional invulnerability — Frigg extracted oaths from all things in existence not to harm Baldr; mistletoe was overlooked and became his death. An imposed protective condition, not an intrinsic racial trait — *Gylfaginning*

**Heimdallr**
- `[Specialization-major]` Born of nine mothers simultaneously — a unique origin among all Æsir, conferring exceptional sensory range (hears grass growing, wool growing on sheep; sees hundreds of leagues) and constitution requiring almost no sleep
