# Raganos

> See also: [raganos_baltic_roster.md](raganos_baltic_roster.md)

**Native name / Etymology:** Ragana (Lithuanian/Latvian, singular), Raganos (plural) — same root as the goddess Ragana; the word functions as both the divine name and the common noun for "witch"; etymology: (1) from *regėti* / *redzêt* ("to see, foresee") — the seers; (2) from *rãgas* / *rags* ("horn, crescent") — the horned ones; male practitioner variant: *raganius* (Lithuanian) / *ragainis* (Latvian), attested but secondary in folk sources

**Source Layer:** Baltic

**Primary origin:** Lithuanian and Latvian oral tradition — *dainos* (folk songs), *pasakos/pasakas* (folk tales); Winter Solstice ritual tradition; 16th–18th century Christian missionary and trial records; Krišjānis Barons, *Latvju dainas* (1894–1915); folk epic *Lāčplēsis*, Andrejs Pumpurs, 1888

**Biological Form:** Spirit

**Entity type:** Race/Species

**Gender(s):** Female (primary attestation); male practitioners (*raganius / ragainis*) exist in folk sources but are consistently secondary figures without the full power-range of the female type

## One-line lore summary

The collective of Baltic supernatural witch-beings — inherent seers, shapeshifters, and cosmic balance-enforcers who gather at Winter Solstice under the goddess Ragana, and whose single absolute vulnerability is the animal form they cannot help but take.

## Specializations

- `[major]` Shapeshifting into fixed personal animal forms — each ragana has a stable set of animal forms (owl, toad, sow, goat, serpent, cat, or dog); the forms are consistent per individual and not chosen arbitrarily; in animal form she passes any barrier
- `[minor]` Milk-theft by magical means — can draw milk from distant livestock and nursing women without physical contact; one of the most consistently documented abilities across Lithuanian and Latvian folk sources
- `[minor]` Weather manipulation — can raise and allay local storms; control of wind; not at the level of Ragana (the goddess) but meaningful at the local scale

## Strengths

- Expert herbalist — deep knowledge of plant properties for healing, poison, and magical application
- Prophecy and divination — degree varies by individual; all raganos possess at minimum a sight beyond the ordinary; some can see past and future clearly
- Cursing — can inflict targeted harm: illness, impotence, crop failure, livestock death; precise and difficult to counter without knowing the ragana responsible
- Night-active without penalty; darkness is their native operating condition
- Collective power at Winter Solstice — the gathering under Ragana (the goddess) amplifies individual abilities and enables coordinated action

## Characteristics

- Not born from mortal humans — an inherent supernatural category; the distinction from mortal practitioners of magic is real in folk belief, though sources sometimes blur it
- Daily appearance: typically old women in unremarkable guise; the beautiful-woman form is deployed specifically for seduction and predation
- Do not use broomsticks — flight is achieved by shapeshifting into birds or by riding tree stumps or branches; the broomstick is a Western European motif absent from Baltic sources
- Solitary in daily life; gather collectively at Winter Solstice on hilltops under the goddess Ragana's authority
- Demand first-offerings from communities they tolerate: first egg of spring, first catch of the hunt, butter, cheese, sheep's wool; these are tribute, not gifts
- Cosmic balance-keepers by nature — punish greed, excess, and hoarding in their local territory; this is an intrinsic drive, not a choice

## Faults / Weaknesses

- Animal-form linkage — the critical and defining vulnerability: when a ragana transforms into an animal, harming that animal directly harms the ragana; killing the animal form kills the ragana; she cannot prevent this consequence of her own shapeshifting; the folk method of identifying and defeating a ragana always passes through this vulnerability
- Power diminishes in summer and daylight — not absent, but the winter-night condition is when all abilities operate at full strength
- Required to act against excess — the balance-enforcement drive can be exploited; a ragana who witnesses extreme greed cannot choose to ignore it, making her behavior partially predictable

## Modifiers

- Under the authority of the goddess Ragana — *divine hierarchy; origin: Lithuanian/Latvian folk tradition; raganos gather under the goddess's command at Winter Solstice; the goddess-raganos relationship is sovereign-to-collective*
- Post-Christianization reframing as devil's servants — *imposed cultural overlay; origin: Lithuanian and Latvian Christianization and the resulting witch trials; trial records describe raganos as agents of the Christian devil and cannot be used as reliable guides to pre-Christian belief about their nature, motivation, or moral standing*

## Ecology

**Prey:** Greedy mortals (targeted for balance-enforcement — transformation, consumption, curse); seduced men; first-offerings demanded as tribute from communities within their territory

**Predators:** Humans who identify and strike the animal form — the only reliable folkloric method; organized religious institutions (post-Christianization); Perkūnas-aligned forces

> **Note on sources:** The line between raganos (the supernatural witch-type) and mortal women who practice magic (*raganos* in the common-noun sense) is blurred in virtually all primary sources. Folk belief holds that true raganos are an inherent supernatural category distinct from mortal practitioners, but trial records treat all accused women as the same type. This profile follows the supernatural-type interpretation supported by the pre-Christianization folk material. The animal-form vulnerability motif is the single most consistent trait across all regional variants and is the most reliable element for game design purposes. The *regėti* ("to see, foresee") root etymology is the most widely attested for the name; see Wiktionary: "ragana" [CC-BY-SA].
