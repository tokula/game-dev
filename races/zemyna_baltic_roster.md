# Žemyna — Baltic Roster

> See also: [zemyna_baltic.md](zemyna_baltic.md)

## Command hierarchy

Žemyna holds no formal authority over other beings and commands no subordinates — her nature is immanent rather than sovereign. She does not rule the earth; she is the earth. Sacred grass snakes (*žalčiai*) function as her living avatars and household representatives, but they are not beings under her command so much as expressions of her presence. Velnias governs the realm within the earth that Žemyna constitutes; their relationship is structural complementarity rather than hierarchy. No sub-beings, priests, or divine subordinates are documented as operating under Žemyna's authority in primary sources.

## Named individuals — Primary sources

Žemyna is a singular immanent divine presence. No named subordinate beings or individual manifestations are documented in primary sources. The diminutive forms of her name appear in the ritual context but represent the same being addressed with tenderness, not distinct entities:

| Name | Source | Rank / role | Notes |
|------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Žemyna | Jan Łasicki (1582); Mikalojus Daukša (1595); Matthäus Prätorius (1690) | Earth goddess; household and agricultural protector | Primary form; first documented Lithuanian deity in written records |
| Žemynėlė / Žemelė | Lithuanian *dainos*, agricultural ritual prayers, oral tradition | Diminutive/intimate address form | Not a separate being; the diminutive suffix expresses the tenderness of the household relationship — "dear earth" |
