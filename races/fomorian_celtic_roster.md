# Fomorians — Celtic Roster

> See also: [fomorian_celtic.md](fomorian_celtic.md)

## Command hierarchy

The Fomorians have a loose hierarchy of kings and regional lords; no single central authority governs all Fomorians simultaneously across the full span of invasion-cycle sources. Attested leadership roles, roughly ordered by narrative prominence:

**Balor of the Evil Eye** — paramount war-chief in the Second Battle of Mag Tuired; his death ends organized Fomorian opposition in that cycle  
**Indech mac Dé Domnann** — king; commands the Fomorian war-host in the Second Battle alongside Balor  
**Conand mac Faebar** — regional lord; controls Tor Conaind (Tory Island tower fortress)  
**Morc mac Dela** — field commander; tribute extraction operations against Nemed's people  
**Cichol Gricenchos** — earliest attested leader (Partholón era); predates the Balor cycle by several invasion cycles

No single primary source establishes a unified Fomorian chain of command across all invasion cycles.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Balor**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Eye of Death — Balor's single eye, when opened, kills everything in its line of sight; kept permanently shut by four men raising the lid by a handle; deployed against enemy formations in battle; this ability is unique to Balor among all attested Fomorians (*Cath Maige Tuired*)
- `[Modifier]` Prophetic fate-bond — foretold that his own grandson would kill him; imprisoned his daughter Ethniu on Tory Island to prevent her bearing a son; the imprisonment guaranteed Lugh's birth through the very breach it was meant to prevent — *origin: cosmic fate expressed through druidic prophecy; the evasive measure fulfilled the prophecy*

**Bres**
- `[Modifier]` Half-Tuatha Dé parentage — mother Ériu (Tuatha Dé sovereignty goddess) gave Bres claim to Tuatha Dé kingship; his Fomorian father's allegiances overrode his obligations to his Tuatha Dé subjects — *origin: Ériu's union with Elatha (Lebor Gabála Érenn)*
- `[Fault]` Sovereignty failure and satire-exposure — his unjust rule (denying feasts, enslaving craftspeople, forcing labour without payment) violated every sovereignty principle; Coirpre the Bard's formal satire stripped his kingship without military force; only possible because his misrule was total

**Cichol Gricenchos**
- `[Characteristic]` Earliest attested Fomorian leader in the invasion cycle; active against Partholón's people before the Tuatha Dé Danann appear in the mythological sequence; the text describes him as *gricenchos* (possibly "fearsome" or "grim"), suggesting a distinctive epithet but without further detail

**Elatha**
- `[Characteristic]` "Darkly beautiful" — explicitly described as unusually handsome, distinguishing him from the grotesque norm and marking high status within the Fomorian hierarchy (*Lebor Gabála Érenn*); beauty among Fomorians appears to indicate noble lineage rather than being racial baseline
- `[Characteristic]` Engaged in a romantic liaison with Ériu (Tuatha Dé sovereignty goddess), arriving at her location by sea in a silver vessel; the encounter is described as neither hostile nor forced, and produced Bres — the only attested case of voluntary Fomorian–Tuatha Dé union in a primary source

**Ethniu (Eithlenn)**
- `[Modifier]` Imprisoned in a crystal tower on Tory Island — isolated by Balor from all male contact to prevent the prophecy; Lugh's conception occurred when Cian (or a divine figure, depending on the source variant) breached the tower — *imposed by Balor's fear of the prophesied grandson*
- `[Characteristic]` Mother of Lugh; her Fomorian lineage as Balor's daughter is the prophetically necessary element — the prophecy specified Balor's own *grandson* as his killer, which required a Fomorian woman in the chain

> **Note:** The identity of Ethniu's partner (Lugh's father) varies across source variants: some name Cian (a mortal or semi-divine figure), others give a divine or unnamed father. The primary sources agree on Ethniu as mother and Balor as grandfather; the paternal line is inconsistent.

**Indech mac Dé Domnann**
- `[Characteristic]` Described in warrior terms as king commanding the Fomorian war-host; no individual supernatural ability is attributed to him in primary sources; his role is organizational command alongside Balor's personal combat power

**Octriallach**
- `[Strength]` Strategic infrastructure targeting — commands a Fomorian detachment to fill the Tuatha Dé battlefield healing spring with stones, each warrior carrying one large stone from the River Lóchét; the operation neutralizes Dian Cecht's overnight battlefield restoration and is the most effective Fomorian tactical counter in the entire Second Battle of Mag Tuired (*Cath Maige Tuired*)
- `[Characteristic]` Namesake cairn — the stone formation left by his operation is recorded as *Carn Octriallach*, one of the few topographical features in *Cath Maige Tuired* named for a Fomorian sub-commander rather than a battle event

**Tethra**
- `[Characteristic]` Sea-lord by kenning — "mag Tethrach" (Tethra's plain) is an Old Irish poetic kenning for the sea; "cattle of Tethra" is a kenning for fish; his name carried sufficient cultural weight as a sea-domain to enter Old Irish poetic tradition (*Sanas Cormaic*; *Lebor Gabála Érenn*)
- `[Characteristic]` Sword Orna — after the Second Battle of Mag Tuired, Ogma of the Tuatha Dé recovered Tethra's sword and drew it from its sheath; the sword recounted the deeds it had performed (*Cath Maige Tuired*); one of the few individually named Fomorian artifacts in primary sources

> **Note:** A secondary-scholarship claim that Tethra presided over the dead of both sides after battle was investigated and found not to be directly attested in accessible PD primary texts — it appears to be a modern inference from his sea and otherworld associations. Only PD primary-attested traits are retained here.
