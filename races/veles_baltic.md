# Vėlės

> See also: [veles_baltic_roster.md](veles_baltic_roster.md)

**Native name / Etymology:** Vėlė (Lithuanian, singular), Vėlės (plural); Latvian *velis* — from Proto-Baltic *\*wēlā*, "spirit, shade of the dead"; cognate with the divine name Velnias (lord of the dead) and the Slavic god Veles, though these are distinct entities; the root carries senses of both "the dead" and "cattle/herd" in comparative Baltic-Slavic study

**Source Layer:** Baltic

**Primary origin:** Lithuanian oral tradition — *raudos* (lamentations) and *dainos* (folk songs); Vėlinės feast ethnographic accounts; earliest written reference in Jan Łasicki, *De diis Samagitarum caeterorumque Sarmatarum et falsorum Christianorum* (1582)

**Biological Form:** Spirit

**Gender(s):** Male and Female — Vėlės are the souls of all dead humans regardless of sex; they retain their appearance and identity from life

## One-line lore summary

The transparent shades of all Lithuanian dead — every person who dies becomes one — dwelling in Velnias's far realm and sustained by the offerings and remembrance of the living.

## Specializations

- `[minor]` Can traverse the boundary between the realm of the dead and the living world — primarily during the Vėlinės feast (early November); outside that ritual window, neglected or wronged Vėlės may still manifest
- `[minor]` Vėlės who suffered unjust death or improper burial can manifest as active presences capable of harming the living

## Strengths

- Retain full memory and personality from their mortal life
- Can serve as protective ancestors to living descendants if properly honored with offerings
- Continue relationships with previously deceased family members in the afterlife realm

## Characteristics

- Shadow-like, transparent as fog, soft as wool — no physical substance
- Retain the appearance, face, and clothing of the deceased — recognizable by those who knew them
- Dwell in the realm of Velnias, described as "very far away, on a high hill," surrounded by deceased relatives
- The vėlė is not solely a post-death phenomenon; in Baltic belief it exists alongside the living person as a companion protective spirit and departs the body at death
- Require periodic food and fire offerings from the living; during Vėlinės, families prepare a feast and openly invite the dead back to the house for warmth and sustenance
- The Vėlinės feast (early November) is the primary occasion when the boundary between realms thins and Vėlės can be directly communicated with and fed

## Faults / Weaknesses

- Without proper burial rites the vėlė becomes restless and may haunt the living as a malevolent presence
- Those who were wronged in life may return with harmful intent regardless of offerings
- Cannot sustain themselves without offerings from living descendants — neglected Vėlės may grow resentful or fade

## Modifiers

- Governed by Velnias (lord of the dead) — *divine authority; Velnias rules the realm in which Vėlės dwell and receives them at death*
- Bound to the ritual calendar — the Vėlinės feast is a contractual occasion in which both the living and the dead hold mutual obligations — *folk-religious compact; origin: Lithuanian mortuary tradition*

## Allies / Rivals / Enemies

| Faction | Standing | Notes | Source |

## Ecology

**Prey:** N/A — spirit beings; sustained by food and fire offerings from living descendants during the Vėlinės feast and other mortuary rites

**Predators:** N/A — the realm of the dead is their natural domain; their vulnerability is neglect by the living, not predation

> **Note on sources:** Lithuanian Vėlės (souls of the dead, plural of *vėlė*) are a distinct concept from the Slavic god Veles/Volos, though both names share a Proto-Baltic-Slavic root. The Vėlės are a universal collective of individual spirits — every human dead — not a deity; the deity who rules them is Velnias (Lithuanian) / Vels (Latvian). Conflating Velnias with the Vėlės, or the Vėlės with the Slavic Veles, is a frequent error in secondary and popular sources.
