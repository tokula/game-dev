# Ljósálfar — Norse Roster

> See also: [ljosalfar_norse.md](ljosalfar_norse.md)

## Command hierarchy

No internal hierarchy among the Ljósálfar is documented in primary sources. Álfheimr is held by Freyr (Vanir/Æsir god) as its ruler — an external authority above the race. No Ljósálfar king, chieftain, council, or internal rank structure is named or described in any surviving Norse text.

**Source priority:** Primary sources (folklore, mythology, religious texts, historical accounts) are the authoritative origin. Games and fiction derive from them. Always populate `— Primary sources` first and most completely. Derivative sections exist for cross-reference only — they should never displace or reframe the original cultural record.

**Section naming rule:** Used `— Primary sources` because the Ljósálfar have a real-world Norse mythological base.

## Named individuals — Primary sources

No individually named Ljósálfar appear in any surviving Norse primary source. The Poetic Edda and Prose Edda invoke them exclusively as a collective (*Ljósálfar*, *álfar*) — no individual is distinguished by name, deed, or attribute in the surviving record.
