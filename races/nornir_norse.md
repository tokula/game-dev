# Nornir

> See also: [nornir_norse_roster.md](nornir_norse_roster.md)

**Native name / Etymology:** Nornir (Old Norse: plural; singular *norn*; etymology uncertain — possibly from a root meaning "to twist, spin" related to fate-weaving, or from *norna* "to warn"; cognate with German *Norne*)

**Source Layer:** Norse

**Primary origin:** Norse mythology — Poetic Edda (anonymous, c. 10th–13th century) and Prose Edda, Snorri Sturluson (c. 1220)

**Biological Form:** Spirit

**Gender(s):** Female — all attested Nornir in primary sources are female or presented as feminine.

## One-line lore summary

Weavers of fate who determine the destiny and lifespan of every being in the Nine Worlds — not a biological race, but a cosmic function filled by individuals drawn from multiple races.

---

> **Classification note:** The Nornir are **not a race** in the biological or tribal sense. *Fáfnismál* (Poetic Edda) states explicitly: *"Most sundered in birth I say the Norns are / They claim no common kin. / Some are of the Æsir, / Some are of the elves, / Some are daughters of Dvalin."* The Nornir are a **cosmic role** — fate-weavers and destiny-carvers — filled by individuals of different racial origins. They function as a profession or cosmological office rather than a species.

## The three primary Nornir

The principal Nornir — Urðr, Verðandi, and Skuld — reside at the Well of Urðr at the base of Yggdrasil. Their names encode the three temporal dimensions of fate:

- **Urðr** — *"What once was"* (past of Old Norse *verða*, "to become")
- **Verðandi** — *"What is becoming"* (present participle of *verða*)
- **Skuld** — *"What ought to / shall be"* (from Old Norse *skulu*, expressing obligation and future necessity)

They water the roots of Yggdrasil from the Well of Urðr, carve runes into the World Tree, and weave the fates of all living beings — including the Æsir. No being within the Nine Worlds can override their decrees.

Beyond the three principal Nornir, *Gylfaginning* states that many Nornir exist and attend every birth across the Nine Worlds, assigning fate to each new life. Some of these individual Nornir are described as good, others as evil — accounting for lives of fortune versus misfortune.

## Specializations

- `[signature]` **Fate sovereignty** — The Nornir's decrees bind all beings including the Æsir; even Odin's foreknowledge cannot change what the Nornir have woven. This is the highest operative authority in Norse cosmology.
