# Valkyrie

**Native name / Etymology:** Valkyrja (Old Norse: "chooser of the slain", from *valr* "those slain in battle" + *kjósa* "to choose"; Proto-Germanic *wala-kuzjōn-*; Old English *Wælcyrie*)

**Source Layer:** Norse

**Primary origin:** Norse mythology — Poetic Edda (*Völuspá*, *Grímnismál*, *Darraðarljóð*) and Prose Edda (10th–13th century)

**Biological Form:** Humanoid

**Common archetype/role in fiction:** Warrior culture, Divine servants

> See also: [valkyrie_norse_roster.md](valkyrie_norse_roster.md) — named individuals and command hierarchy

## One-line lore summary

Odin's divine shield-maidens who ride over battlefields to choose which warriors die, then escort the fallen to Valhalla to prepare for Ragnarök.

## Specializations

- `[signature]` Planar mobility: move freely between Midgard, Asgard, and Valhalla without aid or conveyance
- `[major]` Protective battle magic: can shield chosen warriors and steer combat outcomes
- `[major]` Shape-shifting: some Valkyries wear swan or raven feather cloaks that grant full animal form
- `[major]` Supernatural speed — their mounts far exceed anything mortal horseflesh can achieve
- `[minor]` Prophetic battle-sight: can perceive which warriors are fated to die before combat concludes; some accounts link this to destiny-weaving like the Norns

## Strengths

- Fierce mounted warriors skilled with spears, riding horses, wolves, or boars

## Characteristics

- No independent power base; status and authority derive entirely from Odin's mandate
- Constrained by wyrd (Norse fate); cannot alter cosmologically predetermined outcomes

## Faults / Weaknesses

- Capacity for human emotion (love, jealousy) compromises battlefield impartiality and can be exploited

## Modifiers

- Bound to Odin's authority; defying his commands results in divine punishment — *Brynhildr was cursed to enchanted sleep for saving the wrong warrior against Odin's explicit decree*

## Game depictions (variants from the source mythology)

### God of War Valkyries

Depicted as once-noble warriors cursed and imprisoned by Odin, inverting their mythological role as willing enforcers of his will. The player liberates them rather than opposing them as agents of divine order. Sigrun transitions from the hardest boss encounter to an NPC ally in *Ragnarök* — a significant departure from their source function as unquestioned battlefield executors.

### Honkai Impact 3rd Valkyries

"Valkyrie" is entirely recontextualized as a rank within the fictional Schicksal organization. Valkyries fight extradimensional threats called Honkai using stigmata-enhanced combat suits; the Norse cosmological role (soul selection, Valhalla escort, Ragnarök preparation) is absent. The name is aesthetic rather than mythologically grounded.

### Valkyria Chronicles Valkyria

Renamed "Valkyria" to signal deliberate divergence. These are a nearly extinct humanoid bloodline with ancient supernatural power (the Valkyrian Flame) in an alternate-history Europe analog. No connection to Odin, Valhalla, or Norse cosmology; the race functions as a fantasy lineage with military and political stakes.

---

> **Note on sources:** Norse sources vary on the number and composition of Valkyries. The *Völuspá* names six; *Grímnismál* implies thirteen; other sources list up to 29 named individuals. Some scholars argue the earliest conception was of malevolent death demons (*valkyrjur* in a darker sense) who feasted on battlefield corpses, later euhemerized into noble divine servants under Odin — this darker origin layer is largely absent from popular fiction depictions.
