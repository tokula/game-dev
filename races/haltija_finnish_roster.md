# Haltija — Finnish Roster

> See also: [haltija_finnish.md](haltija_finnish.md)

## Command hierarchy

Haltija have no internal hierarchy among themselves — each is autonomous within its specific domain. Their vertical structure is defined by the major nature deities above them:

- **Ukko** (sky god, chief deity) — supreme over all Finnish spirits
  - **Tapio** (forest deity) — presides over all forest-domain haltija
    - **Mielikki** (forest goddess, Tapio's consort) — governs forest creatures and their guardian spirits
      - **Metsänhaltija** (forest guardian haltija) — individual forest spirits subordinate to Tapio/Mielikki
  - **Kotihaltija** (household spirits) — domain-bound; no superior spirit explicitly named in sources
  - **Vedenhaltija** (water spirits) — domain-bound guardians of specific water bodies
  - **Maan haltija** (land/field spirits) — domain-bound guardians of farmland

No formal hierarchy exists among haltija of the same type; each operates independently within its own territory.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Etiäinen**
- `[Specialization-major]` Exists as a temporal precursor to its human counterpart — its presence, sound, or shadow manifests before the human physically arrives; no other haltija subtype operates in advance of its domain
- `[Characteristic]` Ambiguous ontological status: sometimes described as the person's own spirit sent ahead, sometimes as an independent guardian spirit

**Aarni**
- `[Specialization-minor]` Produces a distinctive blue-flame or will-o'-wisp luminescence visible only above buried valuables; this light is intrinsic and non-transferable
- `[Fault]` Tied strictly to the physical location of buried treasure; cannot range beyond it
