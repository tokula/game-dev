# Haltija — Finnish Roster

> See also: [haltija_finnish.md](haltija_finnish.md)

## Command hierarchy

Forest haltijat follow the most extensively documented internal structure:

```
Tapio (king of forests; rules over all forest life and the Tapiola realm)
  └── Mielikki (mistress of forests; Tapio's consort; governs healing and hunt outcome;
                  in practice prayed to more often than Tapio)
        ├── Tellervo (daughter; forest maiden, associated with clouds and grain)
        ├── Tuulikki (daughter; lesser documented)
        └── Nyyrikki (son; patron of hunters; marks paths through the forest)
```

No equivalent hierarchy is documented for household (*kotihaltija*), sauna (*saunatonttu*), water (*vedenhaltija*), or land (*maanhaltija*) types — each such haltija governs its domain independently with no attested superior.

> **Note:** Tapio and Mielikki occupy a position at the boundary between race-members and deity-tier beings. They are documented as haltijat (place-governing spirits) of the highest order, but also worshipped directly and invoked by name in Kalevala incantations. For game design purposes they can be treated as god-tier NPCs within the Haltija category rather than representative player-character archetypes.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Tapio**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Personifies and governs the entire forest realm (Tapiola) — authority over all forest life, not merely a local domain; this exceeds the standard haltija domain-bonding.
- `[Modifier]` Referred to only by euphemistic hunter's titles in ritual speech ("grey-beard", "king of the forest") — direct naming avoided during hunts. — *Finnish hunting folk belief, Suomen kansan vanhat runot*

**Mielikki**
- `[Specialization-major]` Active, proactive healing of injured animals — treats wounded paws, rescues fallen chicks, heals mating injuries in game birds; a healing specialization beyond general domain governance.
- `[Modifier]` Appearance tied to generosity: golden-dressed and beautiful when hunt is to be plentiful; unkempt and disheveled when she withholds game. — *Kalevala*
