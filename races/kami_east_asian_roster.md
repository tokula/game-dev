# Kami — East Asian Roster

> See also: [kami_east_asian.md](kami_east_asian.md)

## Command hierarchy

```
Takamagahara (Heavenly Plain)
└── Amaterasu Ōmikami (supreme authority)
    ├── Tsukuyomi-no-Mikoto (moon; separated from Amaterasu after a quarrel)
    ├── Susanoo-no-Mikoto (storms; exiled to earth after misconduct)
    └── Ninigi-no-Mikoto (sent to govern the earthly realm; ancestor of imperial line)
        └── [Imperial lineage — human]

Earthly realm (governed by Kunitsukami before heavenly kami descended)
└── Ōkuninushi-no-Kami (master of the earthly realm; ceded authority to Amatsukami)
    └── Sukunabikona-no-Kami (partner; later departed to Tokoyo)

Primordial / Pre-hierarchical
├── Izanagi-no-Mikoto (creator; retired after sealing Yomi)
└── Izanami-no-Mikoto (creator; died; now rules Yomi)
```

Individual kami not under a clear commander — such as Takemikazuchi, Fūjin, Raijin, and Inari — operate within their specific domains under the general authority of Amaterasu but are not depicted as reporting to an intermediate commander in the primary sources.

**Source priority:** Primary sources (folklore, mythology, religious texts, historical accounts) are the authoritative origin. Games and fiction derive from them. Always populate `— Primary sources` first and most completely.

**Section naming rule:** `— Primary sources` is used here because kami have a real-world Shinto religious base documented from 712 CE.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

Only traits that deviate meaningfully from the kami category baseline are listed.

**Amaterasu Ōmikami**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Supreme authority over all Amatsukami and the divine order of Takamagahara; her decree governs celestial succession; the imperial line descends from her through Ninigi — no other kami holds this systemic authority
- `[Specialization-major]` Three sacred imperial treasures (*sanshu no jingi*) — the mirror Yata no Kagami, the jewel Yasakani no Magatama, and the sword Kusanagi-no-Tsurugi — are expressions of her authority passed to the imperial line as governing instruments
- `[Characteristic]` Withdrew into the Ama-no-Iwato cave when Susanoo's rampage caused devastation; her withdrawal plunged the world into darkness — demonstrates emotional reactivity atypical of a sovereign; was lured out by Ame-no-Uzume's comic ritual dance

**Izanagi-no-Mikoto**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Co-creator of the Japanese islands (*ōyashima*) and most of the major kami; the scope of this creative act exceeds any other individual kami's documented output
- `[Specialization-major]` Sealed Yomi — rolled the Chigaeshi-no-Ōkami (great barrier stone) over the entrance to the underworld to separate the living world from the dead; established the fundamental cosmological boundary
- `[Strength]` Ritual purification origin — emerged from Yomi, performed *misogi* purification, and generated new kami from his discarded clothing and cleansed body; this act produced Amaterasu, Tsukuyomi, and Susanoo from his eyes and nose

**Izanami-no-Mikoto**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Co-creator of the Japanese islands and most kami; the most comprehensive creative act in the *Kojiki* cosmology; her labour directly produced Amaterasu, Susanoo, and the major nature kami
- `[Fault]` Death through birth — the only major creator kami to die; killed by the heat of Kagutsuchi (fire kami) during childbirth; became a decaying ruler of Yomi thereafter — death is permanent in her case despite kami resilience
- `[Modifier]` Pollution of Yomi — her body decays after death, transforming her into a *shikome* (underworld demoness); she cannot return to the living world without breaking the taboo against the dead being seen by the living — *Kojiki* (712 CE)

**Susanoo-no-Mikoto**
- `[Specialization-major]` Storm mastery — commands seas and storms with destructive force; capable of flooding rice paddies and demolishing sacred structures with his wrath
- `[Modifier]` Exile from Takamagahara — banished by Amaterasu after uprooting her sacred rice paddies and defiling her weaving hall; the exile is a divine decree, not a self-chosen condition — *Kojiki* (712 CE)
- `[Specialization-major]` Slayer of Yamata no Orochi — the only kami who defeated the eight-headed serpent; lured it with sake, decapitated all eight heads, and discovered Kusanagi within the central tail

**Ōkuninushi-no-Kami**
- `[Specialization-major]` Builder and organiser of the earthly realm — collaborated with Sukunabikona to develop medicine, agriculture, and sorcery for human use before ceding the land to the Amatsukami; the most developed administrative kami of the earthly world
- `[Characteristic]` Multiple resurrections — killed twice by jealous brothers and revived each time through divine intervention; atypical even for kami
- `[Strength]` Master healer — jointly developed *mononoke* remedies and pharmacological knowledge with Sukunabikona

**Tsukuyomi-no-Mikoto**
- `[Modifier]` Estrangement from Amaterasu — killed the food kami Uke Mochi in disgust at how she prepared a meal (generating food from her body orifices); Amaterasu declared she would never look upon him again; the separation of sun and moon is the consequence — *Nihon Shoki* (720 CE)
- `[Characteristic]` Near-absence from the Kojiki — despite being named alongside Amaterasu and Susanoo, Tsukuyomi has almost no narrative role in the primary Kojiki account; Nihon Shoki contains the Uke Mochi episode

**Sukunabikona-no-Kami**
- `[Specialization-minor]` Exceptionally small form — rides ocean waves in a bean-pod boat; identified as the son of Kamimusubi; described as tiny even by kami standards
- `[Strength]` Co-developed medicine and agriculture alongside Ōkuninushi; credited with establishing the foundations of healing practice for humanity
- `[Characteristic]` Voluntary departure — completed his purpose in the earthly realm and departed across the sea to Tokoyo (the eternal land) without warning or farewell; his withdrawal is unprompted and unexplained

**Kuebiko**
- `[Specialization-major]` Omniscience — explicitly described as knowing all things that happen under heaven and on earth; the only kami in the primary sources to be characterised as knowing all things
- `[Fault]` Immobility — cannot walk; power is entirely cognitive and oracular; manifests as a motionless scarecrow; requires others to act on the knowledge he provides
