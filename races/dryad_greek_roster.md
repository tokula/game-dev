# Dryads / Hamadryads — Greek Roster

> See also: [dryad_greek.md](dryad_greek.md)

## Command hierarchy

No formal hierarchy governs Dryads or Hamadryads. Each is sovereign over her own tree or grove. The Meliae, as the eldest class of tree nymphs (born in the aftermath of Uranus's castration, before the Titans), hold ancestral seniority but no authority over younger Dryads. Individual groves may be under the protection of a patron Olympian (Apollo, Artemis, or Pan), whose authority the resident Dryads respect.

**Source priority:** Hesiod's *Theogony* and *Works and Days*, the *Homeric Hymn to Aphrodite*, and Ovid's *Metamorphoses* are the primary sources.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Meliae**
- `[Characteristic]` Pre-Olympian origin — the Meliae are born directly from Uranus's spilled blood; they predate the Titans and exist as a consequence of the first cosmological act of violence; their ash-tree nature connects them to the *Works and Days* tradition of the "iron race" born from ash
- `[Specialization-minor]` Generative ancestry — in some readings, the Meliae are the mothers of the iron race of men (the current humans), born from ash trees per *Works and Days* ll. 145–146; this would make them the direct progenitors of mortal humanity

**Hamadryads of Erysichthon**
- `[Fault]` Existentially vulnerable through the tree — the single most explicit primary-source description of Hamadryad vulnerability: the tree bleeds when struck, the Hamadryad cries out as she dies, and she invokes Demeter with her final breath; the episode establishes definitively that a Hamadryad dies with her tree — *Metamorphoses*, Ovid, Bk 8
- `[Specialization-minor]` Divine connection — the dying Hamadryad's appeal to Demeter is answered; Demeter takes the Hamadryad's cause and punishes Erysichthon; tree nymphs have a direct channel to the divine authority over harvests and natural growth

**Chrysopelia**
- `[Modifier]` Companion bond — after Arcas saves her tree, Chrysopelia enters a permanent bond with him; the gratitude-bond between Hamadryad and benefactor is an unusual but attested variant of the Hamadryad-mortal relationship, inverting the typical antagonistic or indifferent dynamic — *Bibliotheca*, Apollodorus
