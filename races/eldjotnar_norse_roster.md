# Eldjötnar — Norse Roster

> See also: [eldjotnar_norse.md](eldjotnar_norse.md)

## Command hierarchy

Surtr stands supreme over the Eldjötnar as their ruler and champion. No internal hierarchy below Surtr is documented in primary sources; the fire giants are described collectively as his army marching at Ragnarök. The Eldjötnar as a whole acknowledge no authority above them — they are the native inhabitants of Múspellsheim, one of the primordial realms, and owe no documented fealty to the Æsir or any other divine power.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Surtr**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Carries a flaming sword that shines brighter than the sun — no weapon of the Æsir is described as its equal (*Gylfaginning*)
- `[Specialization-major]` Sole figure who survives the mutual destruction of Ragnarök long enough to cast fire over the entire world, burning it to nothing (*Gylfaginning*)

**Logi**
- `[Characteristic]` Personification of uncontrolled wildfire rather than domestic hearth fire — his consuming nature is literal: in the eating contest he devoured not just meat but bones and the wooden trencher as well (*Gylfaginning*)
- `[Characteristic]` Separate genealogical line via Fornjótr — brother of Ægir (sea) and Kári (wind), suggesting an elemental sibling triad distinct from the Múspellsheim fire giants proper

**Sinmara**
- `[Characteristic]` Attested only in Fjölsvinnsmál; her role as keeper of Lævateinn (a weapon crafted by Loki, locked in a chest with nine locks) implies a guardianship function unique among named fire-giant figures; identity remains contested
