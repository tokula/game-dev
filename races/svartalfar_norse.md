# Svartálfar

> See also: [svartalfar_norse_roster.md](svartalfar_norse_roster.md)

**Native name / Etymology:** Svartálfar (Old Norse: *svartr* "black" + *álfar* "elves"; plural of *svartálfr*; meaning "Black Elves." The root *svartr* is cognate with English *swart* and German *schwarz* "black/dark.")

**Source Layer:** Norse

**Primary origin:** Norse mythology — Prose Edda, Snorri Sturluson (c. 1220), *Skáldskaparmál* (primary references); Svartálfheimr mentioned as a distinct realm in *Gylfaginning*

**Biological Form:** Humanoid (disputed — see Sources note)

**Gender(s):** Not specified in primary sources.

**Common archetype/role in fiction:** Scholarly (as craftsmen, if equated with Dvergar)

## One-line lore summary

Black Elves whose name and realm (Svartálfheimr) appear in the Prose Edda — a deeply contested category that many scholars believe is Snorri Sturluson's alternate name for the Dvergar (dwarves) rather than a distinct race.

## Characteristics

- Svartálfheimr ("Black Elf Home") is listed as a distinct realm in the Prose Edda, but it is situated in or near Niðavellir — the documented realm of the Dvergar
- When Loki visits Svartálfheimr in *Skáldskaparmál*, the beings he encounters and contracts are identified as dwarves (Sindri and Brokkr) — no separately described "Black Elf" beings appear in the episode
- The conflation of Svartálfar with Dvergar may originate with Snorri's systematic ordering of the Norse cosmological realms rather than reflecting an older tradition

---

> **Note on sources:** The identity of the Svartálfar is one of the most contested questions in Norse mythological scholarship. Three positions exist in the literature:
>
> 1. **Svartálfar = Dvergar** — Snorri used "Black Elves" as an alternate name for dwarves; Svartálfheimr is the same as Niðavellir; the two terms are synonymous. This is supported by the *Skáldskaparmál* episode where Loki enters Svartálfheimr and immediately contracts with dwarves, encountering no separate Black Elf beings.
>
> 2. **Svartálfar = Dökkálfar** — "Black Elves" and "Dark Elves" are two names for the same underground non-dwarf race. Snorri created unnecessary confusion by using both terms in different contexts.
>
> 3. **Svartálfar are a third distinct category** — genuinely separate from both Dökkálfar and Dvergar. Held by fewer modern scholars due to the absence of supporting primary evidence for a distinct population.
>
> For game design purposes: choose one interpretation explicitly and flag the ambiguity. Cross-reference [dvergar_norse.md](dvergar_norse.md) and [dokkalfar_norse.md](dokkalfar_norse.md) as the two likeliest primary-source referents.
