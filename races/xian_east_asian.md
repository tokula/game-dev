# Xian

**Native name / Etymology:** 仙 / 仙人 (Xiān / Xiānrén — the character 仙 is traditionally analysed as 人 "person" + 山 "mountain", denoting a person who retreats to the mountains to cultivate; an older variant 僊 depicts a person dancing or ascending; English glosses include "immortal", "transcendent", and "adept")

**Source Layer:** East Asian

**Primary origin:** Chinese Daoist tradition; attested from the Warring States period (c. 300–280 BCE) in the Zhuangzi; systematised in the Baopuzi (c. 320 CE) and the Zhongli Chuandao ji (Song dynasty, 960–1279 CE); secondary Buddhist framework in the Śūraṅgama Sūtra (c. 7th–8th century CE)

**Biological Form:** Humanoid

**Entity type:** Race/Species

**Gender(s):** Male and Female — both genders are well-attested in primary sources; the canonical Eight Immortals include He Xiangu alongside seven males; the gender of Lan Caihe is explicitly ambiguous in Ming-dynasty sources

**Common archetype/role in fiction:** Divine servants, Scholarly, Wandering mystic

## One-line lore summary

Transcendent immortals who have escaped death through Daoist or Buddhist cultivation, inhabiting a five-grade hierarchy that bridges mortal earth and the heavenly court of the Jade Emperor.

## Specializations

- `[signature]` Biological immortality — aging halts at the moment of ascension; the Xian is immune to natural death; only karmic regression or heavenly punishment can reverse this state
- `[major]` Qi mastery — can sense, shape, project, and weaponize qi (vital energy) with a depth of attainment that no mortal or Yaoguai reaches through ordinary cultivation alone; encompasses combat applications, healing, and environmental manipulation
- `[major]` Flight — middle and upper grades travel unassisted through the sky via cloud, crane, or sword; attested in Xiyouji and Fengshen Yanyi
- `[minor]` Transformation — Shénxiān and Tiānxiān grades can alter appearance, size, or apparent species; lower grades lack this capacity

## Strengths

- Superhuman endurance and pain tolerance developed through decades or centuries of physical cultivation practice
- Heightened perception of spiritual entities, qi flows, and hidden truths invisible to mortals
- Centuries of accumulated cosmological knowledge and alchemical expertise

## Characteristics

- Five-grade hierarchy (Zhongli Chuandao ji, Song dynasty):
  - 鬼仙 Guǐxiān (Ghost Immortals) — excess yin cultivation; incomplete ascension; lowest grade; may inadvertently drain life essence from nearby beings
  - 人仙 Rénxiān (Human Immortals) — balanced yin-yang; no longer age or fall ill, but retain hunger and thirst; still earth-bound
  - 地仙 Dìxiān (Earth Immortals) — pure yang transformation; transcend physical needs; dwell on blessed mountains or islands such as Penglai
  - 神仙 Shénxiān (Spirit/Divine Immortals) — acquire shape-shifting; accumulate merit toward celestial promotion; remain earth-bound until merit threshold is met
  - 天仙 Tiānxiān (Celestial Immortals) — highest grade; travel between realms; may serve in the heavenly bureaucracy under the Jade Emperor
- Cultivation lineage — every Xian carries a master-disciple bond or school affiliation; these obligations do not dissolve after ascension
- Dietary transcendence (辟穀 bìgǔ, grain avoidance) — middle and upper grades subsist on qi, elixirs, air, dew, or celestial food rather than ordinary sustenance; lower grades may still require normal food during active cultivation
- Sacred anchoring — each Xian is associated with a specific mountain, grotto, blessed island, or celestial bureau; prolonged displacement from that site is spiritually disorienting
- Dual-tradition cultivation origin — Daoist methods (inner alchemy 內丹 nèidān, external alchemy 外丹 wàidān, qi breathing, dietary regimen) and Buddhist meditative asceticism are both documented entry paths; the Śūraṅgama Sūtra frames xian primarily as practitioners of physical and ingestion methods rather than samadhi, positioning them below the Buddhist cosmological ideal

## Faults / Weaknesses

- Inner demons (心魔 xīnmó) — unresolved grief, desire, or rage manifests as spiritual adversaries that can destroy accumulated cultivation from within; this vulnerability is intrinsic to the Xian's nature as ascended mortals who carry their full emotional history into immortality
- Karmic regression — accumulating sufficient bad karma can forcibly demote a Xian by one or more grades or strip immortality entirely; attested in Fengshen Yanyi
- Mortal attachment — deep emotional bonds to the living world create openings for spiritual attack or voluntary regression; the more intense the attachment, the greater the vulnerability

## Modifiers

- Heavenly mandate (天命 Tiānmìng) — celestial Xian assigned to the Jade Emperor's court are bound by heavenly law; transgression triggers divine punishment, demotion, or exile into mortal reincarnation — *Jade Emperor's celestial court; attested in Xiyouji and Fengshen Yanyi*
- Sworn brotherhood oath (義盟 Yìméng) — binding mutual obligations between groups of Xian with spiritual consequences for oath-breaking; a voluntarily accepted condition, not a permanent species trait — *ritual oath; attested across Ming-era Daoist literature*

## Allies / Rivals / Enemies

| Faction | Standing | Notes | Source |
|---------|----------|-------|--------|
| Jade Emperor (玉皇大帝) and the Heavenly Court | Superior | Celestial Xian are assigned duties, regulated, and judged by the heavenly bureaucracy; lower grades are largely autonomous | *Xiyouji*; *Fengshen Yanyi* |
| Yaoguai (妖怪) | Rival / Enemy | Xian frequently act as exorcists and moral arbiters over Yaoguai; some Yaoguai pursue their own cultivation and earn Xian status; others actively contest Xian authority | *Xiyouji*; *Fengshen Yanyi* |
| Dragon Kings (龍王) | Ally | Cooperation widely documented; Dragon Kings serve the heavenly court alongside Xian | *Xiyouji* |

## Ecology

**Prey:** N/A — middle and upper grades practice bigu (grain avoidance) and subsist on qi, elixirs, or celestial food; Ghost Immortal grade may drain life essence, but this is a manifestation of incomplete cultivation (see Faults), not conventional predation

**Predators:** N/A — no natural predators; inner demons (xīnmó) function as the internal "predator" of cultivation; Heavenly Enforcers are an institutional threat for Xian who violate heavenly law

---

> **Note on sources:** The five-grade hierarchy (Guǐxiān through Tiānxiān) derives from the Song-dynasty *Zhongli Chuandao ji*, not from the earlier *Baopuzi*, which uses a different three-tier scheme (Celestial, Earthly, and Corpse-Untie immortals). The two systems are not directly equivalent. The *Shenxian Zhuan* is traditionally attributed to Ge Hong (c. 320 CE), but scholarly consensus places its actual composition in the 6th–8th centuries CE; it is PD regardless of the true date.
