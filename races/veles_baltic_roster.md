# Vėlės — Baltic Roster

> See also: [veles_baltic.md](veles_baltic.md)

## Command hierarchy

Two parallel authorities govern the vėlės in Lithuanian cosmology:

- **Velnias** (underworld god) — shepherd of the dead; the vėlės browse as his flock in lush chthonic meadows; the etymological connection (PIE *\*welh₃-*) is intrinsic to their relationship
- **Vėjopatis** (Lord of the Wind) — governs the gate to the celestial *Dausos*; determines which souls enter; the psychopomp **Auštaras** guides good souls to Dausos under Vėjopatis's authority; harmful souls are blown into oblivion

The vėlės themselves have no internal hierarchy — they are a universal category (every deceased person) rather than a ranked corps. Their primary social unit remains the family: dead parents oversee living children; ancestors maintain connection to their own family line.

## Named individuals — Primary sources

No named individual vėlė is attested in primary sources examined. Basanavičius's *Iš gyvenimo vėlių bei velnių* (1903–1904) is the primary written repository and may contain named figures within its approximately 7,000 texts, but those were not accessible in this research. The Vėlės tradition names the class and its governing deities, not individual members.
