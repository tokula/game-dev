# Yōkai — East Asian Roster

> See also: [yokai_east_asian.md](yokai_east_asian.md)

## Command hierarchy

No unified hierarchy governs yōkai as a category. Yōkai are autonomous entities whose authority is local and situational — a powerful individual may command lesser spirits in its territory, but no single ruler over all yōkai is attested in primary sources.

Within specific groups, hierarchy is documented:
- **Ōeyama oni (Taiheiki, c. 1370):** Shuten-dōji commands approximately 1,500 oni subordinates from his stronghold at Ōeyama; his lieutenants include named oni such as Ibaraki-dōji
- **Hyakki yagyō:** The night procession implies collective movement but no named commander is attested in primary sources

**Source priority:** Primary sources (folklore, mythology, religious texts, historical accounts) are the authoritative origin. Games and fiction derive from them. Always populate `— Primary sources` first and most completely. Derivative sections exist for cross-reference only — they should never displace or reframe the original cultural record.

**Section naming rule:** `— Primary sources` is used here because yōkai have a real-world folkloric and literary base predating all modern adaptations.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

Only traits that deviate meaningfully from the yōkai category baseline are listed here.

**Yamata no Orochi**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Eight heads and eight tails — vastly exceeds baseline yōkai morphology; body described as spanning eight hills and eight valleys
- `[Characteristic]` Occupies the kami / yōkai boundary — described in *Nihon Shoki* as a powerful local entity rather than a categorised monster; later tradition reclassifies it as yōkai; its original status is ambiguous even in the source

**Shuten-dōji**
- `[Specialization-major]` Military command — organises and leads a force of approximately 1,500 oni, a level of social organisation unique among individually named yōkai in the primary sources
- `[Strength]` Exceptional physical resilience — continues to fight and attempts to bite his attackers after decapitation; requires multiple heroes to overcome
- `[Fault]` Susceptibility to sake — his fondness for drinking is weaponised against him; the heroes disguise poisoned sake as a gift, which strips his supernatural defences before the killing blow (*Taiheiki*, c. 1370)

**Kiyohime**
- `[Specialization-major]` Full serpentine transformation — transforms into a massive serpent capable of swimming a river in pursuit and melting the bronze bell of Dōjōji temple with her flames to reach the monk hiding inside
- `[Modifier]` Transformation is induced by betrayal and unrequited passion, not by innate yōkai nature — she is born human; the intensity of her emotion crosses a threshold that converts her into a supernatural being — *Konjaku Monogatarishū* (c. 1120 CE)
