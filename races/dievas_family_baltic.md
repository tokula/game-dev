# Dievas Family

> See also: [dievas_family_baltic_roster.md](dievas_family_baltic_roster.md)

**Native name / Etymology:** Lithuanian: *Dievo šeima* ("God's family"), from *Dievas* (supreme sky god) + *šeima* (family). Latvian: *Dievu ģimene*. *Dievas* derives from Proto-Indo-European \**Dyēus* / \**deiwos* ("celestial, shining sky"), root \**dyew-* — cognate with Sanskrit *devá*, Latin *deus*, and Old Norse *Týr*.

**Source Layer:** Baltic (Lithuanian and Latvian)

**Primary origin:** Baltic pre-Christian mythology — primarily attested in Lithuanian and Latvian dainas (folk songs), 19th-century compilations; secondarily in Jan Łasicki's *De diis Samagitarum* (1582/1615) and Matthäus Prätorius's *Deliciae Prussicae* (c. 1690s).

**Biological Form:** Spirit

**Entity type:** Race/Species

**Gender(s):** Male and Female — Dievas and the Dievo sūneliai are consistently male; Saulė and Aušrinė are consistently female; Mėnulis is male.

**Common archetype/role in fiction:** Divine servants, Warrior culture

## One-line lore summary

The celestial household of Dievas, Baltic sky-father — an immortal family of divine horsemen, light-bearers, and cosmic laborers who govern sky, sun, moon, and dawn from their farmstead on the silver mountain.

## Specializations

- `[signature]` Cosmic stewardship — collectively maintain celestial order: the Dievo sūneliai pull Saulė's chariot across the sky daily, Aušrinė kindles the dawn fire, Mėnulis governs the night sky; the cycle of light, seasons, and time depends on their unceasing labor.
- `[major]` Divine horse form — the Dievo sūneliai (Ašvieniai) manifest as white horses with golden manes, possessing infinite stamina; they move at the speed of the sun across the sky.
- `[minor]` Cross-realm navigation — family members can pass between sky, sea, and land: the sons sail a golden boat to rescue Saulė from the sea; Aušrinė appears as star, maiden, or mare depending on the realm.

## Strengths

- Inexhaustible endurance — the sons pull the celestial chariot daily without tiring.
- Skilled celestial craftsmen and farmers — plow, harrow, and tend the sky-mountain farmstead.
- Mastery of cosmic navigation across sky, sea, and land realms.
- Radiant presence — members of the family emit or carry divine light (Saulė as living sun, Aušrinė as morning star).

## Characteristics

- Immortal and ageless; dwell on a silver mountain in the sky where they maintain a working farmstead (fields, barns, a sauna).
- Operate as an extended household rather than a war-band — celestial labor (plowing, herding, lighting fires) is as central to their identity as divine authority.
- The family drama of the cosmos — marital conflict, infidelity, and punishment among family members explains natural phenomena (lunar phases, the separation of sun and moon in the sky).
- Non-hierarchical in daily life despite Dievas holding supreme authority — the sons, daughters, and associated deities each govern their own domain with considerable autonomy.

## Faults / Weaknesses

- Subject to divine justice — even family members are not immune to punishment by Perkūnas; Mėnulis was disfigured for infidelity; cosmic law supersedes family loyalty.
- Dievas's passivity — the sky-father holds supreme authority but rarely intervenes directly; crises in the divine household are resolved by Perkūnas rather than Dievas himself.

## Modifiers

- Dievas's non-intervention principle — *cosmic disposition attested across dainas and Prätorius; the sky-father's authority is upheld through established order rather than direct action*

---

> **Note on sources:** The "Dievas family" is not named as a collective in any single primary source — the concept emerges from the cumulative picture of the dainas, where Dievas, his sons, Saulė, and Aušrinė consistently inhabit the same celestial farmstead and interact as a household. Saulė maintains her own separate household with her daughters (Saulės dukros / Saules meitas) — whether she belongs to the Dievas family or is a neighboring allied deity is ambiguous across sources; both framings appear in different dainas. The relationship between Dievas and Perkūnas also varies: some dainas treat Perkūnas as Dievas's son, others as an independent deity of equal standing — this file treats them as allies of equal standing per the majority of attested material.
