# Kaukas

> See also: [kaukas_baltic_roster.md](kaukas_baltic_roster.md)

**Native name / Etymology:** Kaukas (Lithuanian), plural Kaukai; diminutive Kaukutis; alternate name Barstukai when acting under the direct patronage of Puskaitis — etymology uncertain; possibly from a Proto-Baltic root meaning "little being"; also identified with *Pūkis* in some sources, though that term overlaps with the separate Aitvaras spirit

**Source Layer:** Baltic

**Primary origin:** Lithuanian oral tradition — folk belief texts and household ritual practices; earliest written reference in Jan Łasicki, *De diis Samagitarum caeterorumque Sarmatarum et falsorum Christianorum* (1582), which describes kaukai as "little men who eat vegetarian food" and connects them to Velinas

**Biological Form:** Spirit

**Gender(s):** Male and Female — males more common in primary accounts; both attested

## One-line lore summary

Tiny Lithuanian household spirits of fertility and wealth who dwell in the homesteads they choose, multiplying abundance in exchange for grain tributes — and reversing every blessing the moment those tributes are withheld.

## Specializations

- `[minor]` Can transform into a handful of dust and reconstitute — passage between solid and dispersed form is instantaneous
- `[minor]` Passive preservation magic — food and household goods last measurably longer than natural in any home they inhabit

## Strengths

- Bring abundant harvests, growing wealth, and household prosperity to their chosen homestead
- Watch over and protect household members from harm
- Very small size makes them effectively invisible in daily life
- Can perform minor helpful tasks around the homestead

## Characteristics

- Gnome-like appearance with grain-toned complexions ranging from golden wheat to dark rye
- Very small stature — described consistently as tiny men
- Vegetarian diet — eat only plant-based food; early sources specifically note this
- Live in or regularly visit a chosen homestead (barns, cellars, under thresholds)
- Early sources identify them with the souls of the dead, connecting them to Velnias's domain
- When operating under Puskaitis's patronage, also called Barstukai — these specifically influence earth fertility and harvest yields
- Require a midnight feast left in the barn to maintain goodwill; the offering is a contractual tribute, not a gift

## Faults / Weaknesses

- Require ongoing tribute — if the household grows greedy or forgets offerings, the Kaukas reverses all accumulated blessings and may depart entirely
- Tied to the fortunes of the homestead — if the household collapses, the Kaukas loses its purpose and station
- Cannot be summoned or relocated against their will; the choice of home is entirely theirs

## Modifiers

- Under the authority of Puskaitis (patron deity living underground beneath the elder bush) — *divine hierarchy; Kaukai serve as Puskaitis's earthly agents of fertility*
- Connected to the realm of Velnias through their association with the souls of the dead — *mythological identification; origin: Łasicki (1582)*

## Allies / Rivals / Enemies

| Faction | Standing | Notes | Source |
|---------|----------|-------|--------|
| Puskaitis | Superior | Patron deity of Kaukai/Barstukai; lives underground beneath the elder bush; directs their fertility and harvest work | Lithuanian folk belief |
| Velnias (underworld god) | Complex | Early sources equate Kaukai with the souls of the dead — placing them under Velnias's domain; relationship not described as hostile | Łasicki (1582) |
| Host household | Charge | The Kaukas is guardian and enricher of its chosen household; its departure signals household ruin | Lithuanian folk belief |

## Ecology

**Prey:** Vegetarian — grain, vegetables, and food offerings left by the household at midnight in the barn

**Predators:** Neglectful or greedy households (the primary existential threat is withdrawal of tribute, which causes departure or reversal of blessings)
