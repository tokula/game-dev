# Peckols — Baltic Roster

> See also: [peckols_baltic.md](peckols_baltic.md)

## Command hierarchy

Peckols is the sole ruler of the Old Prussian underworld. He holds equal but separate cosmic authority alongside Perkunos (sky) and Patrimpos (earth-surface) in the divine triad — no hierarchy is formally established between the three; each is supreme in his domain. Below Peckols, no named subordinate beings are documented in primary sources. His sacred fire was maintained by a priestly class (*criwe* or *criway*) who served as intermediaries between the living and the divine triad, but these are human priests, not supernatural beings under his authority.

## Named individuals — Primary sources

Peckols is a singular divine being. No named sub-beings or named individual manifestations are documented in primary Old Prussian sources.

| Name | Source | Rank / role | Notes |

## Sources

### Primary sources

- *Sudovian Book* (Sūduvių knygelė), c. 1520s — lists Peckols and Pockols as two distinct entities
- *Constitutiones Synodales*, 1530 — equates Peckols with Pluto
- 1418 Memorandum to Pope Martin V
- Matthäus Prätorius, *Deliciae Prussicae*, 1690 — largely derivative of Grunau
- Konstantinas Sirvydas, *Dictionarium Trium Linguarum*, 1629 — links Peckols to Baltic cognates
- Simon Grunau, *Preussische Chronik*, 1517–1529 — reliability heavily contested; source of the "Patollo" pseudomythologeme
