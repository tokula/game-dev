## Command hierarchy

```
Jade Emperor (玉皇大帝 Yù Huáng Shàngdì) — supreme authority over all celestial Xian
  └── 天仙 Tiānxiān (Celestial Immortals) — highest grade; serve in heavenly bureaux; travel between realms
        └── 神仙 Shénxiān (Spirit/Divine Immortals) — shape-shifting; earth-bound until sufficient merit earned
              └── 地仙 Dìxiān (Earth Immortals) — transcend physical needs; dwell on blessed mountains and islands
                    └── 人仙 Rénxiān (Human Immortals) — no longer age or sicken; still hunger and thirst; earth-bound
                          └── 鬼仙 Guǐxiān (Ghost Immortals) — lowest grade; incomplete cultivation; excess yin
```

*Grades defined by the Zhongli Chuandao ji (Song dynasty, 960–1279 CE). The Jade Emperor's court sits above the hierarchy — Celestial Xian serve under it; lower grades operate independently.*

## Notable groups

### The Eight Immortals (八仙 Bā Xiān)

The canonical group of eight whose collective identity crystallised in Yuan–Ming dynasty literature. Individual figures are attested as far back as Tang and Song dynasty texts. The group travels together and is often depicted crossing the sea using their individual emblems as vessels (八仙過海 Bā Xiān guò hǎi, "the Eight Immortals cross the sea"). Each represents a distinct social archetype: soldier, scholar, official, recluse, beggar, youth, ambiguous-gender wanderer, and woman.

Sworn brotherhood (義盟 Yìméng) binds the group: their mutual obligations have spiritual force, and cooperation between them is documented as both a practical alliance and a formal oath relationship.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

Only individuals who deviate meaningfully from the racial template are listed here.

**Han Zhongli 漢鍾離 (Zhongli Quan)**
- `[Specialization-major]` Doctrinal authorship — attributed co-transmitter of the definitive five-grade Xian taxonomy via the *Zhongli Chuandao ji*; no other Xian holds a comparable founding-textual role in the tradition

**Lü Dongbin 呂洞賓**
- `[Specialization-minor]` Sword-flight — his "celestial sword" (天遁劍) is a specific weapon-based flight technique distinct from the cloud-riding available to higher grades generally
- `[Modifier]` Quanzhen patriarchhood — revered as second patriarch of the Quanzhen Daoist school (Song–Yuan dynasty); this institutional role imposes ongoing obligations and attributions not shared by most Xian — *Quanzhen school formation, Song–Yuan dynasty*

**Zhang Guolao 張果老**
- `[Characteristic]` Backward-riding — deliberately rides his donkey facing rearward; a sustained eccentricity that signals rejection of conventional social orientation; unique among the Eight Immortals and unremarked in the race profile
