# Sidhe (Aos Sí) — Celtic Roster

> See also: [sidhe_celtic.md](sidhe_celtic.md)

## Command hierarchy

The Sidhe have no single overarching king after the Milesian settlement. Authority is distributed among the lords of specific mounds, with Manannán mac Lir holding the broadest domain — he controls access to the otherworld as a whole and governs the sea passage to Tír na nÓg. The Dagda originally allocated the mounds (*De Gabáil in t-Sída*); Bodb Derg, son of the Dagda, is attested in later tradition as inheriting the Dagda's de facto leadership of the Tuatha Dé after his death.

**Manannán mac Lir** — sea god and guardian of otherworld passage; controls the boundary between mortal Ireland and the sídhe as a whole; no mortal reaches the otherworld without traversing his domain  
**Bodb Derg** — lord of Síd Bodb; successor to the Dagda as de facto king of the assembled Tuatha Dé/Sidhe in later tradition  
**Aengus (Mac ind Óc)** — lord of Brú na Bóinne (Newgrange); domain of youth, love, and the most sacred mound  
**Midir** — lord of Brí Léith; sídhe of County Longford; pursues mortal dealings across lifetimes

No unified military or political command above the individual mound-lord level is documented in primary sources for the post-settlement era.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Aengus (Mac ind Óc)**
- `[Specialization-minor]` Brú na Bóinne acquisition — secured permanent residence in Ireland's most sacred mound by asking the Dagda for "a day and a night," which in Irish idiom encompasses all time; whether this is a genuine supernatural ability or a contractual cleverness is left open by *De Gabáil in t-Sída*
- `[Characteristic]` His sídhe domain is the Neolithic mound of Newgrange — the oldest and most cosmologically significant of the sídhe; his occupation of it distinguishes him from lords of smaller or less storied mounds

**Bodb Derg**
- `[Characteristic]` Successor authority — emerges in later primary tradition as the leading figure among the assembled Tuatha Dé following the Dagda; his sídhe at Síd Bodb functions as the closest equivalent to a court for the post-settlement Sidhe (*De Gabáil in t-Sída*)
- `[Characteristic]` Son of the Dagda — his lineage positions him as the natural heir to the Dagda's role as organizational centre of the Tuatha Dé; no primary source explicitly states his succession but his prominence in later texts reflects it

**Manannán mac Lir**
- `[Specialization-major]` Otherworld passage control — governs the boundary between the mortal world and the sídhe; can grant or deny access to the otherworld for mortals and divine beings alike; his domain encompasses the sea as the physical medium of passage
- `[Characteristic]` Absent from both battles of Mag Tuired; his power operates in the domain of travel, boundary, and passage rather than military conflict; he is the primary figure mortals encounter when crossing into the otherworld rather than when encountering the Sidhe in the mortal world

**Midir**
- `[Specialization-major]` Cross-reincarnation recognition — identifies Étaín in her mortal reincarnation as a human queen despite her complete amnesia; no other primary-source Sidhe lord is attested perceiving a divine soul across human lifetimes (*Tochmarc Étaíne*)
- `[Specialization-minor]` Magical fidchell compact — the paradigmatic attested case of a Sidhe lord using a formal board-game challenge to create a supernatural debt binding on a mortal king; the obligation persists even when the king refuses to honor it, authorizing Midir's transformation-flight escape (*Tochmarc Étaíne*)
- `[Characteristic]` Brí Léith — his named mound establishes the pattern of sídhe as geographically discrete lordships with non-overlapping territories; his domain is distinct from Aengus's Brú na Bóinne and from the Dagda's holdings (*De Gabáil in t-Sída*)
