# Giltinė — Baltic Roster

> See also: [giltine_baltic.md](giltine_baltic.md)

## Command hierarchy

Giltinė operates beneath Laima (the fate goddess) in the Lithuanian cosmic hierarchy. Laima determines the hour and manner of each mortal's death; Giltinė executes the decree without discretion or delay. She in turn commands the Maro deivės ([maro_deives_baltic.md](maro_deives_baltic.md)) as agents of mass death, directing their passage from town to town. No internal sub-hierarchy is documented beneath Giltinė herself.

## Named individuals — Primary sources

Giltinė is a singular divine being; no named subordinate death-spirits appear individually in primary Lithuanian sources. The following regional variant names may represent alternate manifestations or local epithets of the same archetype:

| Name | Source | Rank / role | Notes |
|------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Giltinė | Lithuanian *dainos*, oral tradition | Death goddess, primary form | The canonical form; sister of Laima in most accounts |
| Kaulinyčia | Regional Lithuanian folk belief | Alternate manifestation or local epithet | Means "bone woman"; may be a distinct local death spirit or a regional name for Giltinė |
| Maro mergė | Folk narratives | Plague-maiden aspect | Sometimes used for Giltinė when she manifests as a plague-spreader; overlaps with the Maro deivės collective |
