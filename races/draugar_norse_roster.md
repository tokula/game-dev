# Draugar — Norse Roster

> See also: [draugar_norse.md](draugar_norse.md)

## Command hierarchy

None — each draugr is an autonomous revenant bound to its own burial mound or territory. No collective leadership or hierarchy is attested. The most powerful draugar command de facto authority over their immediate area through fear and physical dominance, but there is no documented relationship between individual draugar.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Glámr**
- `[Specialization-major]` Could cast a dying curse on his killer — Grettir was cursed with permanent night-blindness and an irrational fear of darkness from the moment Glámr's eyes met his. This capacity to curse one's killer is not documented for draugar generally — *Grettis saga*
- `[Specialization-major]` Physical strength that exceeded even Grettir, one of the strongest men in saga tradition; Grettir only prevailed by preventing Glámr from pinning him and using surprise to decapitate him — *Grettis saga*
- `[Modifier]` Rose as a draugr due to improper death (killed by a malevolent spirit while violating a taboo) and refusal to remain dead — *Grettis saga*

**Þórólfr bægifótr**
- `[Specialization-major]` His draugr-presence caused livestock death across an entire district, drove farmhands to madness, and killed directly through his rides; the scale of environmental harm exceeds the typical individual-threat pattern — *Eyrbyggja saga*
- `[Modifier]` Rose as a draugr because of his malevolent and violent nature in life, which continued and amplified after death — *Eyrbyggja saga*

**Víga-Hrappr**
- `[Specialization-minor]` Attested shapeshifter — took the form of a seal, which is unusual even among draugar — *Laxdæla saga*
- `[Modifier]` Demanded burial upright under his own doorway so he could continue to watch over his household — an unusual burial request that preserves the connection between the draugr and its territory — *Laxdæla saga*

**Angantýr**
- `[Characteristic]` Retains enough residual honour and paternal feeling to respond to his daughter Hervor's summons and yield the cursed sword Tyrfingr to her, despite warning her of the consequences — an unusually reasoned response for a draugr — *Hervarar saga ok Heiðreks*
- `[Modifier]` Bound in death to the sword Tyrfingr, which he was buried with; could not rest while it remained in the mound — *Hervarar saga ok Heiðreks*
