# Dvergar — Norse Roster

> See also: [dvergar_norse.md](dvergar_norse.md)

## Command hierarchy

No formal internal hierarchy is attested in primary sources. The Dvergar act as individual craftspeople or pairs (e.g. Fjalarr and Galarr; Brokkr and Sindri). In the dvergatal of *Völuspá*, Mótsognir is named first among dwarves and Durinn second — they may represent primordial or founding figures — but no leadership structure or authority chain is defined. The Dvergar as a collective operate under the structural authority of the Æsir above them.

**Source priority:** Primary sources (folklore, mythology, religious texts, historical accounts)
are the authoritative origin. Games and fiction derive from them. Always populate
`— Primary sources` first and most completely. Derivative sections exist for cross-reference
only — they should never displace or reframe the original cultural record.

**Section naming rule:** Use `— Primary sources` when the race has a real-world folkloric or

entirely by the IP filter and never reach this label. The label signals whether you're citing
a cultural tradition or an open creative work.

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Álvíss**
- `[Specialization-major]` Recites the name of every object in all Nine Worlds in every language spoken by gods, elves, dwarves, giants, and the dead — attested in dialogue with Thor in *Álvíssmál*
- `[Fault]` Petrified by dawn sunlight after Thor's deliberate riddle-delay — the primary direct attestation of Dvergar sunlight vulnerability in surviving texts

**Brokkr and Sindri**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Forged Mjölnir, Draupnir, and Gullinbursti simultaneously in one wager; Mjölnir's short handle is the sole acknowledged flaw, caused by Loki's interference — *Skáldskaparmál*

**Andvari**
- `[Modifier]` His ring Andvaranaut carries a death-curse on all who possess the gold, placed by Andvari at the moment of forced surrender — *Skáldskaparmál*; *Völsunga saga*

**Fjalarr and Galarr**
- `[Specialization-minor]` Brewed the mead of poetry from Kvasir's blood — the only beings documented to have done so — *Skáldskaparmál*

**Regin**
- `[Strength]` Reforged the shattered sword Gramr for Sigurðr, making it stronger than the original — *Völsunga saga*
- `[Modifier]` Driven by desire for Fáfnir's gold; orchestrates Sigurðr's deeds to recover it; his treacherous intent is revealed to Sigurðr through Fáfnir's blood-wisdom — *Völsunga saga*
