# Dvergar — Norse Roster

> See also: [dvergar_norse.md](dvergar_norse.md)

## Command hierarchy

No centralised authority over all Dvergar is documented in primary sources. Individual dwarves operate as independent craftsmen, sometimes in workshop partnerships (Sindri and Brokkr; the sons of Ívaldi). The four cardinal dwarves (Norðri, Suðri, Austri, Vestri) hold permanent cosmological positions supporting the sky but have no governing authority over other Dvergar.

**Source priority:** Primary sources (Poetic Edda, Prose Edda) are authoritative. All individuals listed here are attested in those texts.

## Named individuals — Primary sources

| Name | Source | Rank / role | Notes |
|------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Sindri (also Eitri) | Prose Edda (*Skáldskaparmál*) | Master craftsman | Forged Mjölnir, Draupnir, and Gullinbursti (Freyr's golden boar) in a wager against Loki; Loki in fly form bit his brother Brokkr during forging, causing Mjölnir's handle to be slightly short |
| Brokkr | Prose Edda (*Skáldskaparmál*) | Master craftsman; Sindri's brother | Worked the bellows for Sindri's forge; Loki's sabotage (biting Brokkr as a fly) explains Mjölnir's shortened handle |
| Sons of Ívaldi | Prose Edda (*Skáldskaparmál*) | Craftsmen workshop | Forged Gungnir (Odin's spear), Skíðblaðnir (Freyr's folding ship), and Sif's golden hair as a replacement for what Loki cut; the individual names of the sons are not preserved in primary sources |
| Norðri, Suðri, Austri, Vestri | Prose Edda (*Gylfaginning*) | Cosmological pillars | The four cardinal dwarves holding the four corners of the sky — North, South, East, West; their stations are permanent cosmological functions assigned at creation |
| Fjalarr | Prose Edda (*Skáldskaparmál*) | Brewer; murderer | With Galarr, killed Kvasir (a being of pure wisdom, made from divine accord) and brewed the Mead of Poetry from his blood; also killed the giant Gilling and his wife for sport |
| Galarr | Prose Edda (*Skáldskaparmál*) | Brewer; murderer | Co-conspirator with Fjalarr; Suttungr (Gilling's son) later captured them both and forced surrender of the mead as blood-price |

## Individual distinctions — Primary sources

**Sindri (Eitri)**
- `[Specialization-signature]` Forged Mjölnir — the defining protective weapon of Norse cosmology and the primary instrument upholding cosmic order against Jötnar assault; no craftsman of any other race has produced an equivalent

**Fjalarr and Galarr**
- `[Fault]` Predatory violence beyond the Dvergar baseline — the murder of Kvasir (a being created from the mingled goodwill of both divine tribes) and the gratuitous killing of Gilling and his wife establishes these individuals as extreme even by Dvergar moral standards

**Andvari**
- `[Modifier]` Cursed gold — the treasure Andvari surrendered to Loki carries his spoken curse; once transferred, the curse travels with the gold independently of Andvari, but its invocation here launches one of the central cursed-object chains in Norse mythology — *Reginsmál*
