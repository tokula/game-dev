# Erebus

**Native name / Etymology:** Ἔρεβος (*Érebos*); from a Proto-Indo-European root *h₁rébʰos meaning "darkness" or "blackness"; cognate with Sanskrit *rájas* (darkness, dimness) and Gothic *riqis* (darkness). Also used in primary sources as a place-name for the underworld region of perpetual darkness beneath the earth.

**Source Layer:** Greek

**Primary origin:** Greek mythology — Hesiod's *Theogony* (c. 700 BCE) is the primary and essentially sole source for Erebus as a deity.

**Biological Form:** Elemental (divine)

**Entity type:** Primordial Being

**Gender(s):** Male

## One-line lore summary

One of the first entities to emerge from Chaos — the living embodiment of primordial darkness — who together with Nyx (Night) preceded all light and gave rise to Aether and Hemera as their first offspring.

## Specializations

- `[signature]` **IS primordial darkness** — Erebus does not inhabit or govern darkness; he is its substance. His existence constitutes the first darkness of the cosmos before light existed. No training, power, or divine status could replicate this ontological identity.

## Characteristics

- Emerged from Chaos alongside Nyx (Night) as one of the first-order entities of the cosmos, predating the Olympians by cosmic generations (*Theogony* ll. 123–125).
- Together with Nyx, produced Aether (the bright upper sky) and Hemera (Day) — the first positive pair, born as opposites of their parents (*Theogony* ll. 124–125).
- His name is also used in primary sources as a place-name: the region of dark shadow beneath the earth, above Tartarus, through which souls pass on their way to the underworld.
