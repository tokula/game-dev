# Ragana

**Native name / Etymology:** Ragana (Lithuanian/Latvian) — dual etymology: (1) from *regėti* / *redzêt* ("to see, foresee") — the prophetic seer; (2) from *rãgas* / *rags* ("horn, crescent") — the horned or lunar one; the word also functions as the common noun for "witch" in both languages — the divine name and the common noun share the same form

**Source Layer:** Baltic

**Primary origin:** Lithuanian and Latvian oral tradition — *dainos* (folk songs), *pasakos / pasakas* (folk tales), and Winter Solstice ritual tradition; Krišjānis Barons, *Latvju dainas* (1894–1915); 16th–18th century Christian missionary and trial records

**Biological Form:** Spirit

**Entity type:** Singular Deity

**Gender(s):** Female

**Common archetype/role in fiction:** Corrupted/exiled (from the perspective of the Indo-European sky-deity layer that displaced her)

## One-line lore summary

The pre-Indo-European Baltic witch-goddess of death, rebirth, prophecy, and cosmic balance — patron and sovereign of the raganos, enforcer of equilibrium between the living and the dead.

## Specializations

- `[signature]` Cosmic balance enforcement — compulsively intervenes when excess accumulates; transforms, consumes, or punishes mortals who accumulate too much wealth, power, or lifespan; an embedded nature, not a choice
- `[major]` Shapeshifting — transforms into owl, toad, sow, goat, serpent, cat, or dog; no evident limit on sustained duration in animal form
- `[minor]` Resurrection — some folk traditions attribute to her the power to return the dead to life; not universally attested across all sources

## Strengths

- Absolute prophetic sight — foresight not limited by time or distance within the mortal realm
- Master healer — the same knowledge that enables cursing enables healing at equivalent depth
- Weather control — raises and allays storms; controls local wind patterns
- Governs the domain of female biological cycles — fertility, birth, death-transitions
- Commands lesser raganos ([../races/raganos_baltic.md](../races/raganos_baltic.md)) as her handmaidens and agents; gathers them at Winter Solstice

## Characteristics

- Default form: tall old woman with long teeth and gnarled skin
- Seduction form: beautiful woman — used specifically to prey on men; appearance shifts between extremes without intermediate states
- Rides tree stumps or branches — the broomstick is a Western European motif absent from Baltic sources
- Most active in winter and at night; power diminishes but does not vanish in summer and daylight
- Predates the Baltic Indo-European sky-deity pantheon (Perkūnas, Dievas); was never incorporated into it; exists in structural opposition to that layer
- Gathers with raganos collectively at Winter Solstice on hilltops; these gatherings are her primary ritual institution

## Faults / Weaknesses

- Animal-form vulnerability — when in animal form, harm done to that animal is reflected directly back to Ragana; she is most vulnerable while a sustained animal form is maintained
- Compelled by the balance function — cannot ignore extreme excess in her territory; must act against it even when tactically disadvantageous
- Power diminishes in daylight and summer — measurably reduced outside her optimal winter-night conditions

## Modifiers

- Excluded from and structurally opposed by the Indo-European Baltic sky-deity layer — *mythological displacement; the Indo-European sky-deity tradition (Perkūnas, Dievas) arrived in Baltic culture without integrating Ragana; her power exists in the spaces the sky-deity hierarchy does not reach*
- Post-Christianization demonization — *imposed cultural overlay; 16th–18th century Christianization reduced the figure from cosmic balance-keeper to devil's servant; trial records and missionary documents after this point cannot be taken as reliable guides to pre-Christian characterization*

## Ecology

**Prey:** Greedy or excessive mortals — transforms or consumes them as an act of balance enforcement; seduced men; excess accumulation in nature that must be redistributed

**Predators:** Those with knowledge of her animal-form vulnerability; organized religious institutions (post-Christianization); sky-deity-aligned forces (Perkūnas and his agents)

---

> **Note on sources:** "Ragana" functions simultaneously as the name of this specific divine figure and as the common Lithuanian and Latvian word for "witch." Primary sources rarely distinguish between the goddess and lesser raganos; when a folklore text says "a ragana appeared," it may mean the goddess herself or a lesser witch-being. This profile treats Ragana as the divine archetype; the collective of lesser witch-beings is documented separately in [../races/raganos_baltic.md](../races/raganos_baltic.md). Post-Christianization trial records (16th–18th century) describe ragana figures primarily as devil's servants and cannot be used as reliable guides to pre-Christian belief about the goddess. The pre-Indo-European origin claim is a scholarly inference based on the word's resistance to straightforward Indo-European etymology and the figure's absence from the Baltic sky-deity pantheon; it is not directly attested in primary texts.
