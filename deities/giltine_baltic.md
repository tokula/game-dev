# Giltinė

**Native name / Etymology:** Giltinė (Lithuanian); derived from *gelti* ("to sting" or "to bite"). The name encodes the mechanism of her action — death arrives as a sting or venomous strike. Alternative etymological suggestions (*geltonis*, "to wither/fade") exist in secondary literature but the *gelti* derivation is most widely accepted.

**Source Layer:** Baltic (Lithuanian)

**Primary origin:** Lithuanian oral tradition and mythology; earliest written attestation in *De diis Samagitarum* by Jan Łasicki (c. 1582, published 1615); plague-maiden tradition documented in Mickiewicz (1828) and Siemieński (1845)

**Biological Form:** Spirit

**Entity type:** Singular Deity

> **Entity type note:** The skill definition lists Giltinė as an example of a **Cosmological Personification** (a being that *is* a concept rather than inhabiting it; impersonal, non-interactable). However, Lithuanian folk sources consistently depict her as an **active individual agent** — she manifests in physical form (white robe, long tongue), kills by deliberate personal action, is associated with a specific sacred animal (the owl), and can in principle be propitiated. This profile uses **Singular Deity** as the more accurate fit for the attested material. The classification discrepancy is flagged here for transparency.

**Gender(s):** Female

## One-line lore summary

The Lithuanian goddess of death who moves among the living in white robes, kills by applying graveyard poison with her tongue, and is the inevitable agent of each person's final moment.

## Specializations

- `[signature]` **Death-touch via poison tongue** — licks poison from gravestones and applies it to the dying; her touch is the direct mechanism of death, not a side effect of combat or disease
- `[major]` **Inevitable reach** — every mortal's death is mediated through her; she cannot be permanently evaded, only (in folk belief) briefly delayed through ritual or luck

## Strengths

- Moves unseen among the living until the appointed moment
- Associated with plague and pestilence as well as individual death — her domain scales from a single death to mass mortality events

## Characteristics

- Manifests as a **female figure in a white robe**
- Possesses a **long, venomous tongue** used to collect poison from gravestones and deliver death
- Sacred bird: **owl**
- Also identified with plague (sometimes called *Maras*, "the Black Death"), linking her individual death-dealing with epidemic mortality
- Sister of **Laima** (goddess of fate and birth) in some folk sources — life and death as paired divine sisters

## Faults / Weaknesses

None attested — she is the agent of death, not subject to it.

## Modifiers

None attested in primary sources.
